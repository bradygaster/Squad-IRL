## Scenario

Sarah is planning her daughter Lily's 7th birthday party. Lily is obsessed with unicorns and wants "the best party ever" (her words). Sarah has a budget of $400, their backyard for a venue, and 3 hours on a Saturday afternoon (2pm-5pm). She's inviting 12 kids from Lily's class. Sarah works full-time and has limited time to prep—she needs activities that keep kids busy but don't require elaborate setup. She's overwhelmed by Pinterest-perfect party ideas and doesn't know where to start.

## Party Timeline (Minute-by-Minute)

### 1:00pm-1:45pm: SETUP (45 minutes)

**Sarah's Tasks** (enlist partner/friend to help if possible):
- [ ] Hang "Unicorn Crossing" banner on fence (5 min)  
- [ ] Set up activity stations: craft table, treasure hunt start, game area (15 min)  
- [ ] Inflate balloons and tie to mailbox/fence (10 min) - OR skip this, they pop anyway  
- [ ] Set up food table with tablecloth, plates, cups, napkins (5 min)  
- [ ] Pre-portion snacks into bowls (goldfish, pretzels, fruit) (5 min)  
- [ ] Hide treasure hunt clues and prize bags (5 min)  

**PRO TIP**: Set up the day before if possible. Day-of setup is stressful.

---

### 2:00pm-2:20pm: ARRIVAL & FREE PLAY (20 minutes)

**What Happens**:  
Kids arrive (not all at once—expect a 10-15 minute stagger). As they arrive, Lily greets them and they immediately go to the "unicorn coloring station" (low-key activity while waiting for everyone).

**Sarah's Tasks**:
- [ ] Greet parents, collect emergency contact info if needed  
- [ ] Direct kids to coloring station  
- [ ] Hand out unicorn headband party favors as kids arrive (they wear them during party!)

**Station Setup**:
- Folding table with unicorn coloring pages (printed free from online)  
- Crayons in cups  
- "Color your own unicorn!" sign

**Why This Works**: Early arrivals aren't bored waiting, and Sarah isn't scrambling to entertain while greeting parents.

---

### 2:20pm-2:50pm: ACTIVITY 1 - Unicorn Treasure Hunt (30 minutes)

**What Happens**:  
Kids go on a backyard treasure hunt following riddle clues to find the "lost unicorn magic." Final treasure: a bag of chocolate gold coins and unicorn stickers for each kid.

**How It Works**:
1. Sarah gathers kids and reads the first clue aloud  
2. Kids work together to solve it and find the next clue (6 clues total, hidden around the backyard)  
3. Each clue leads to the next, building excitement  
4. Final clue leads to a big "treasure chest" (decorated cardboard box) with individual loot bags for each kid

**Sarah's Prep**:
- [ ] Print treasure hunt clues (provided below—just print and hide!)  
- [ ] Hide clues the morning of the party (takes 5 minutes)  
- [ ] Pre-pack treasure bags: 1 gold coin bag + 1 unicorn sticker sheet per kid (13 bags total)

**Sample Clues** (age-appropriate riddles):
1. "Where the flowers grow and the grass is green, the next clue hides where it can't be seen!" (Answer: flower pot)  
2. "When the sun is hot and you need some shade, look where Lily's swing is made!" (Answer: swing set)  
3. ... (continue with 4 more clues ending at the treasure chest)

**Why This Works**: Group activity (no one's left out), active (kids run around), and requires minimal supervision once they start.

---

### 2:50pm-3:20pm: ACTIVITY 2 - Unicorn Craft Station (30 minutes)

**What Happens**:  
Kids decorate their own unicorn headbands (which they're already wearing!) with stick-on gems, ribbon, and glitter pom-poms. They can also decorate a "unicorn wand" (wooden dowel with ribbon streamers).

**Station Setup**:
- Folding table with newspaper underneath (glitter containment!)  
- Bins of: stick-on gems, glitter glue pens, ribbon scraps, pom-poms  
- Pre-made unicorn horns (foam cones covered in gold glitter paper, attached to headbands)  
- Unicorn wands: wooden dowels with pre-tied ribbon streamers (5-6 colors per wand)

**Sarah's Role**: Float between kids, help with glue gun if needed (for older kids who want to add extra ribbons), manage glitter chaos.

**Why This Works**: Craft doubles as a take-home party favor. Kids love making things they can keep. Seated activity gives them a break from running around.

---

### 3:20pm-3:45pm: ACTIVITY 3 - Unicorn Games (25 minutes)

**Game 1: Unicorn Freeze Dance** (10 minutes)  
- Play upbeat music (Kidz Bop, Disney soundtracks)  
- Kids dance, freeze when music stops  
- Last one moving each round sits out  
- Winner gets to pick the next song  
- **Why it works**: Gets energy out, requires zero setup

**Game 2: Pin the Horn on the Unicorn** (10 minutes)  
- DIY version: Print a large unicorn poster (Staples can print 24x36" for $8), tape to fence  
- Blindfold kids, spin them around, they try to stick a foam horn (with double-sided tape) closest to the unicorn's forehead  
- Mark each attempt with kid's name  
- Winner gets first pick of treasure from treasure chest  
- **Why it works**: Classic party game, easy to manage, kids love it

**Game 3: Unicorn Says** (5 minutes - buffer activity if you have extra time)  
- Like Simon Says, but "Unicorn says..."  
- Commands: "Unicorn says gallop like a horse!" "Unicorn says flap your wings!" "Unicorn says neigh!"  
- **Why it works**: No setup, kills time if an activity runs short

---

### 3:45pm-4:15pm: FOOD TIME (30 minutes)

**Menu** (Easy, kid-friendly, minimal prep):
- **Main**: Pizza (order delivery for 3:30pm arrival) - 3 large pizzas (cheese + pepperoni)  
- **Sides**: Baby carrots, goldfish crackers, pretzels (pre-portioned in bowls)  
- **Drinks**: Juice boxes (12-pack), water bottles  
- **Dessert**: Birthday cake (see below)

**Sarah's Tasks**:
- [ ] Have kids wash hands (hose outside or bathroom in small groups)  
- [ ] Set out pizza on table  
- [ ] Pour drinks (or let kids self-serve juice boxes)  
- [ ] Bring out cake with candles, sing "Happy Birthday"  
- [ ] Cut and serve cake

**Cake**:  
- **Easy Option**: Order from grocery store bakery ($30-40, done by 3pm day-of)  
- **Theme**: Unicorn sheet cake with rainbow frosting and edible glitter  
- **Size**: 1/4 sheet serves 15-20 (plenty for 13 kids + leftovers for Sarah's family)

**Why This Works**: Kids are hungry and tired by now. Eating = 30 minutes of calm. Pizza is universally loved and requires zero cooking from Sarah.

---

### 4:15pm-4:50pm: ACTIVITY 4 - Unicorn Dance Party / Free Play (35 minutes)

**What Happens**:  
Post-cake sugar rush! Turn on music (Spotify "Kids Dance Party" playlist) and let kids dance, play in the yard, or revisit craft station. This is intentionally unstructured "free play" time.

**Sarah's Role**: Supervise, take photos, start sneaking in cleanup (trash bags, wiping tables).

**Optional Add-On**: Bubble machine ($15 on Amazon, kids LOVE this, requires zero effort from Sarah)

**Why This Works**: By this point, kids are happy and tired. They don't need another structured activity. Free play lets them wind down before parents arrive.

---

### 4:50pm-5:00pm: PICKUP & GOODBYES (10 minutes)

**What Happens**:  
Parents start arriving (expect 4:55-5:05pm stagger). Kids grab their craft projects, say goodbye, leave happy.

**Sarah's Tasks**:
- [ ] Hand out thank-you bags (if you're doing them—see optional below)  
- [ ] Take a final group photo with Lily and her friends  
- [ ] Thank parents for coming

**Optional Thank-You Bags**: If you want to send kids home with favors:
- Small organza bags ($10 for 20 on Amazon)

- Cost: ~$2 per bag = $24 total

## What Could Go Wrong (& How to Fix It)

### Problem: It rains ☔
**Solution**: Move everything inside or to a covered patio. Treasure hunt becomes "indoor scavenger hunt" (hide clues in rooms). Free play happens in living room. Pizza still works indoors!

### Problem: A kid gets hurt (scraped knee, bee sting)
**Solution**: Have a first-aid kit ready (Band-Aids, ice pack, antiseptic wipes). Stay calm, clean the wound, call parents if it's anything more than minor. This is why you collected emergency contacts!

### Problem: Two kids fight over a craft supply
**Solution**: "We have enough for everyone! Let's take turns. Lily, can you share the pink gems with Emma?" Have extras of popular items (glitter, gems).

### Problem: An activity flops or ends too early
**Solution**: That's why you have buffer activities (Unicorn Says, bubble machine, free play). If something ends 10 minutes early, pivot to the next thing. No one will notice!

### Problem: Pizza arrives late
**Solution**: Have snacks out (goldfish, pretzels, carrots). Kids can munch while waiting. Call the pizza place at 3:35pm if it's not there yet.

### Problem: A kid doesn't like pizza
**Solution**: Have PB&J ingredients on standby (bread, PB, jelly). Takes 2 minutes to make.

### Problem: Lily has a meltdown (overstimulated, tired, emotional)
**Solution**: This is her special day, and she's 7—meltdowns happen! Take her inside for 5 minutes, give her water, hug her, remind her how fun the party is. Enlist a trusted adult (partner, grandparent) to manage kids outside while you help Lily reset.

## Pro Tips from Parents Who've Been There

✅ **Recruit a Helper**: Ask your partner, friend, or Lily's grandparent to help. One person manages activities, one person handles logistics (food, cleanup, emergencies).

✅ **Bathroom Strategy**: Before treasure hunt and before pizza, announce "Bathroom break! Everyone who needs to go, line up!" Prevents mid-activity interruptions.

✅ **Take Photos, But Don't Stress**: Assign someone to take photos (partner, friend) so you can be present. Or just take 3-4 good shots and enjoy the party.

✅ **Embrace "Good Enough"**: Your party doesn't need to look like Instagram. Kids care about: cake, friends, activities. That's it. Balloons drooping? No one cares. Glitter everywhere? It's a party!

✅ **Have Fun**: Lily will remember that YOU had fun with her, not whether the decorations were perfect.
