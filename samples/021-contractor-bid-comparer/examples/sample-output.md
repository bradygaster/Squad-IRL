## RED FLAGS IDENTIFIED

### 🚨 Bid #1 (Green Tree Plumbing) — AVOID

1. **Missing Permit Costs** — Quote doesn't include $450 Portland permit fee. This is either intentional lowballing or suggests they plan to skip permitting (illegal and will bite you at resale).

2. **Vague Material Specs** — "Contractor-grade PVC" is not a real specification. Schedule 40 PVC is the code-minimum standard. This vagueness suggests they might use thinner Schedule 35 or DWV pipe to cut costs.

3. **50% Upfront Payment** — Demanding $4,250 before work starts is a major red flag. Oregon law limits deposits to $1,000 or 1/3 of contract value (whichever is less). This violates ORS 87.007 and suggests potential fly-by-night operation.

4. **No License Number Provided** — The quote doesn't list a CCB license number. In Oregon, any job over $1,000 requires a licensed contractor. We couldn't verify their license status.

5. **Suspiciously Low Price** — At $8,500, this is 30% below market rate for a legitimate permitted job. Combined with other red flags, this suggests corner-cutting.

**Risk Assessment:** HIGH — This contractor shows multiple signs of unlicensed or unethical work. If they damage your property, you'll have limited recourse.

### ⚠️ Bid #3 (Master Rooter Services) — OVERPRICED

1. **40% Premium Over Fair Market** — $18,000 for 40 feet of residential sewer line is excessive. Portland market rate is $250-$350/foot for permitted work. They're charging $450/foot.

2. **Overbuilt Specifications** — Cast iron pipe is commercial-grade and costs 3x more than Schedule 40 PVC. For a residential lateral, this is overkill and unnecessary expense.

3. **Long Wait Time** — 6-week lead time suggests they're not hungry for work or understaffed. Not inherently bad, but inconvenient if your sewer is backing up.

**Risk Assessment:** LOW — They're expensive but legitimate. You're paying for a Cadillac when a Honda will do the job.

## SPECIFIC SCOPE GAPS TO ADDRESS

Before signing with PDX Excavation & Drain, add these items:

### 1. Video Inspection of Remaining Line
**Why:** Your sewer is 60+ years old. The collapsed section at 40 feet might not be the only problem. Bid #3 includes this ($350 value), but Bids #1 and #2 don't.

**Action:** Ask PDX Excavation to add post-repair video inspection of the entire line from house to street for $300-$400. This will reveal any other weak spots before you backfill.

**Script:** "Can you include a video inspection of the full line after the repair? I want to make sure the rest of the 60-year-old pipe is sound before we close the trench."

### 2. Landscape Restoration Details
**Why:** All three bids mention "backfill and compact" but only Bid #3 specifies topsoil depth (6 inches) and grass seed type (Pacific Northwest blend).

**Action:** Confirm PDX will provide 6 inches of topsoil and seed (not just backfill with excavation spoils). Get this in writing.

**Script:** "Your bid says backfill and restore landscaping. Can you specify that you'll provide 6 inches of topsoil and appropriate grass seed, not just compact the dirt?"

### 3. Utility Locate Confirmation
**Why:** None of the bids mention calling 811 or Oregon Utility Notification Center.

**Action:** Confirm PDX will call for utility locates before digging (required by law). This should be free/standard.

**Script:** "I assume you'll call 811 for utility locates before excavating — can you confirm that's included?"
