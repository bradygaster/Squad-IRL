## Scenario

Emma is flying to Tokyo in 5 days for a 10-day work trip (3 days of client meetings, then 7 days exploring). She's a chronic over-packer who once brought 4 pairs of shoes for a weekend trip. She needs a packing list that accounts for: (1) November Tokyo weather (chilly but not freezing), (2) business-casual meetings then casual sightseeing, (3) fitting everything in a carry-on (no checked bag), and (4) keeping her from packing "just in case" items she'll never use.

**Your mission:** Fit everything in a carry-on so you don't waste 45 minutes at baggage claim.

## 🇯🇵 TOKYO-SPECIFIC REMINDERS

**FIRST DAY IN TOKYO:**
1. Buy Suica card at airport (prepaid subway card)
2. Withdraw yen from 7-Eleven ATM (better rate than airport exchange)
3. Buy portable Wi-Fi device OR SIM card
4. Stop at 7-Eleven for snacks + drinks (it's better than US gas station, trust me)

**CULTURAL NOTES:**
- Remove shoes at temples/ryokans/some restaurants (look for shoe racks at entrance)
- Don't eat while walking (it's considered rude — sit or stand still)
- Bow when greeting people (small nod is fine)
- Don't tip (it's considered rude — service charge is included)
- Trash cans are rare — carry your trash until you find one

**SAFETY:**
- Tokyo is one of the safest cities in the world (but stay alert)
- Keep crossbody bag in front of you on crowded trains
- Emergency number: 110 (police), 119 (ambulance)
