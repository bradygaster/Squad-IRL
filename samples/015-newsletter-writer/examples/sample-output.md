# Newsletter Writer — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Marcus runs a local coffee roastery with 1,200 email subscribers. He has business updates (new Ethiopian single-origin bean, hired a new barista named Sarah, won "Best Local Roastery" award) but his newsletters read like corporate press releases. Current open rate: 18%.

## What the Agents Did

First draft: "We are pleased to announce the addition of a new Ethiopian single-origin to our lineup. This coffee features notes of blueberry and dark chocolate." Boring! Revised version injects personality: "Remember that Ethiopian coffee I've been obsessing over for 3 months? The one I kept bringing back samples of and forcing our entire team to taste-test? It's FINALLY here, and I'm not exaggerating when I say it's the best thing we've ever roasted."

Transformed each business update into a story: Hiring Sarah becomes "Meet Sarah, our newest team member (and the person who can actually make latte art that doesn't look like an asymmetrical blob, unlike SOME of us)." Winning the award becomes "We won Best Local Roastery and honestly, I cried a little. Not a lot. Just a tasteful amount."

## Final Output

Newsletter with Marcus's authentic voice, stories instead of announcements, conversational tone, behind-the-scenes photo of him taste-testing the Ethiopian coffee with an exaggerated expression, and clear CTA: "Try the Ethiopian this week — use code NEWBEAN for 15% off your first bag. Warning: You might become as obsessed as I am." **Open rate jumped from 18% to 42%. 89 people used the discount code in the first 3 days. 4 people replied with variations of "This is the best newsletter I've ever read from a coffee shop."**
