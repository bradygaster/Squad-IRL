# Bedtime Story Generator — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Emma (age 6) loves unicorns and her best friend Zoe. Her mom wants a bedtime story about bravery that's NOT scary. 10-minute read-aloud length.

## What the Agents Did

First draft was 2,100 words (too long) and used words like "trepidation" (too advanced for age 6). The revised story is 987 words, features Emma and Zoe helping a baby unicorn find its family in the Sparkle Forest, teaches bravery through helping others, and uses age-appropriate vocabulary.

## Final Output

**"Emma and the Lost Unicorn"** — A 10-minute adventure where Emma uses her bravery to help a scared baby unicorn named Shimmer find his way back to Rainbow Valley. Emma learns that being brave doesn't mean not being scared — it means helping others even when you are scared. The story ends with Emma and Zoe becoming honorary Protectors of Sparkle Forest, and Shimmer's mom giving them each a golden feather that glows whenever they're brave. Includes 3 discussion questions for parents: "When was a time you were brave?" "How did Emma help Shimmer feel less scared?" "What would you do if you found a lost unicorn?" Mom reports Emma asked for this story 4 nights in a row. ✨
