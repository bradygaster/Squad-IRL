## Scenario

Alex is a 32-year-old software developer who wants to build muscle and lose 15 pounds. He has a home gym (barbell, dumbbells up to 50lbs, pull-up bar, bench) and can train 4 days/week for 60 minutes. He's been doing random YouTube workouts for 6 months with minimal progress. He needs a structured 12-week program with progressive overload, proper rest days, and realistic expectations.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: Initial Program

**Trainer** created a 12-week hypertrophy-focused program with 4 training days/week.

The program included exercises and set/rep schemes, but progression was unclear and volume ramped up too aggressively in Week 3.

**PhysioGuard** scored it **72/100** and found:

- No deload week scheduled (spec requires deload every 4th week to prevent overtraining)
- Week 3 jumps from 12 sets/muscle group to 18 sets — too aggressive for an intermediate lifter
- Several exercises require equipment Alex doesn't have (cable machine, leg press)
- Warm-up and cool-down sections are missing
- No guidance on RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) or when to increase weight
- Rest periods aren't specified for each exercise type

### Round 2: Refined Program

**ProgressTracker** addressed the feedback:

- Added deload weeks (Week 4, Week 8, Week 12)
- Smoothed volume progression: 10 sets → 12 sets → 14 sets → deload
- Replaced cable/machine exercises with dumbbell/barbell alternatives Alex can do at home
- Added warm-up (5-10 min) and cool-down (5 min) for every session
- Included RPE targets (7-8 for working sets) and clear progression guidelines (+5lbs when hitting top of rep range at RPE 7-8)
- Specified rest periods (60-90s for hypertrophy work, 2-3min for compound lifts)

# **12-Week Hypertrophy Program for Alex**
**Goal:** Build muscle + lose 15 lbs | **Equipment:** Barbell, dumbbells (up to 50lbs), pull-up bar, bench | **Training Days:** 4x/week

## **PROGRAM OVERVIEW**

**Training Split:** Upper/Lower (2x/week each)
- **Monday:** Upper Body A
- **Tuesday:** Rest or light cardio/walk
- **Wednesday:** Lower Body A
- **Thursday:** Rest or light cardio/walk
- **Friday:** Upper Body B
- **Saturday:** Lower Body B
- **Sunday:** Rest

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4):** Build work capacity, learn movement patterns  
**Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8):** Increase volume and intensity  
**Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12):** Peak volume, then deload and reassess  

**Progressive Overload Strategy:**
- When you hit the top of the rep range at RPE 7-8 for all sets, increase weight by 5 lbs next session
- RPE Scale: 7 = could do 3 more reps, 8 = could do 2 more reps, 9 = could do 1 more rep, 10 = failure
- Track every workout (weight, reps, RPE) in a notebook or app

**Deload Weeks:** Week 4, Week 8, Week 12  
- Reduce volume by 40% (fewer sets)
- Keep intensity moderate (RPE 6-7)
- Focus on technique and recovery

## **WEEKS 2-12: PROGRESSION PLAN**

### **Phase 1: Weeks 1-3 (Build Base)**
- **Volume:** 16-17 sets per session
- **Progression:** Add 5 lbs when you hit top of rep range at RPE 7-8
- **Focus:** Learn proper form, build work capacity

**Example:** If you bench press 135 lbs for 3x10 at RPE 7, increase to 140 lbs next session.

### **Week 4: DELOAD**
- **Volume:** 10-11 sets per session (40% reduction)
- **Intensity:** RPE 6-7 (lighter weights, focus on form)
- **Purpose:** Recovery, prevent overtraining

**Example Week 4 Monday:**
- Barbell Bench Press: 2x8 at 70% of working weight
- Barbell Row: 2x8 at 70% of working weight
- Dumbbell Overhead Press: 2x10
- Pull-Ups: 2x5
- Skip isolation work or do 1 light set each

---

### **Phase 2: Weeks 5-7 (Increase Volume)**
- **Volume:** 18-20 sets per session (add 1 set to main lifts, add an extra exercise)
- **Progression:** Continue adding weight when hitting top of rep range
- **New additions:**
  - Monday: Add Incline Dumbbell Press (3x10-12)
  - Wednesday: Add Leg Press alternative (Hack Squats with dumbbell: 3x12)
  - Friday: Add Face Pulls with band (3x15-20)
  - Saturday: Add Nordic Curls or stability ball leg curls (3x8-10)

### **Week 8: DELOAD**
- Same structure as Week 4

---

### **Phase 3: Weeks 9-11 (Peak Volume)**
- **Volume:** 20-22 sets per session
- **Progression:** Continue progressive overload; you should be hitting PRs regularly now
- **Intensity:** Some sets can push to RPE 8-9
- **New additions:**
  - Add drop sets on final set of isolation exercises (reduce weight by 30%, go to near-failure)
  - Increase upper body frequency slightly (add 1-2 sets to lagging muscle groups)

### **Week 12: DELOAD + REASSESS**
- Same structure as Week 4 & 8
- **Take progress photos, measurements, and weight**
- **Assess what worked:** Which lifts went up the most? Which muscles grew? Where do you want to focus next?
- **Decide:** Continue this program for another 12 weeks, switch to strength focus, or try a new split

## **NUTRITION GUIDANCE**

To build muscle and lose 15 lbs over 12 weeks:

**Target:** Lose ~1-1.5 lbs/week (this allows muscle growth while losing fat)

**Calories:** Start at maintenance - 300 calories/day  
- Track for 2 weeks, adjust if not losing 1-1.5 lbs/week
- Use an app like MyFitnessPal or MacroFactor

**Protein:** 1g per lb of bodyweight (if Alex weighs 200 lbs → 200g protein/day)  
- This preserves muscle while in a deficit

**Carbs & Fats:** Fill remaining calories  
- Prioritize carbs around workouts for performance
- Don't go below 50g fat/day (hormonal health)

**Example Day (for 200 lb male at 2,200 calories):**
- Protein: 200g (800 cals)
- Carbs: 200g (800 cals)
- Fats: 65g (585 cals)
- Total: ~2,200 calories

## **COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID**

❌ **Skipping warm-ups** → Increases injury risk  
✅ **Always warm up** — 5-10 minutes gets blood flowing and prepares joints

❌ **Adding weight too fast** → Form breaks down, progress stalls  
✅ **Follow RPE guidelines** — only increase weight when you can hit top of rep range at RPE 7-8

❌ **Skipping deload weeks** → Leads to burnout and injury  
✅ **Deload every 4 weeks** — recovery is when muscles grow

❌ **Not eating enough protein** → Can't build muscle in a deficit without sufficient protein  
✅ **Hit 1g/lb bodyweight** — non-negotiable for body recomposition

❌ **Doing cardio before lifting** → Depletes energy for strength work  
✅ **Cardio after lifting or on rest days** — 20-30 min walks are perfect
