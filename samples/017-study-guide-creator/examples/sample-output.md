# Study Guide Creator — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Ava has a Biology 201 exam in 5 days covering 4 dense chapters: cellular respiration, photosynthesis, DNA replication, and protein synthesis. Her lecture notes are disorganized, she missed 2 lectures, and she's panicking because she has no idea where to start.

## What the Agents Did

First draft just summarized the textbook chapters in 30 pages of dense paragraphs (not helpful for studying). Revised guide uses active learning strategies: concept maps showing how cellular respiration and photosynthesis are inverse processes, comparison tables for DNA vs. RNA structure, step-by-step visual diagrams for protein synthesis (transcription → translation), and 40 practice questions organized by difficulty with full answer explanations.

Organized by learning priority: Key Concepts You MUST Know (the 20% that accounts for 80% of exam questions), Common Misconceptions (where students typically mess up), Memory Tricks (mnemonic devices like "ATP = adenosine TRI-phosphate = 3 phosphate groups"), and Practice Problems (exam-style questions that mirror professor's testing style based on past exams).

## Final Output

25-page study guide with visual concept maps, side-by-side comparison tables, practice questions with detailed explanations, mnemonic devices for memorizing enzyme names and sequences, concept checks after each section to test understanding, and a realistic 5-day study schedule: Day 1 (cellular respiration + practice Qs), Day 2 (photosynthesis + practice Qs), Day 3 (DNA replication + practice Qs), Day 4 (protein synthesis + practice Qs), Day 5 (full practice exam + review weak areas). **Ava scored 91% on the exam (up from her usual 78-82% range). She said the concept maps alone were worth the entire effort.**
