# Budget Accountability Coach — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Jessica set a budget goal to spend no more than $600/month on "discretionary" spending (dining out, shopping, entertainment). It's February 22nd and she's already at $847. She asked for a gentle budget reminder. She needs tough love and a concrete plan to finish the month strong.

## What the Agents Did

First draft: "You've overspent by $247. Try to cut back this week." Too vague! Revised with actual accountability: "Okay, real talk: You've spent $847 in 22 days. That's $38.50 PER DAY on stuff you don't NEED. At this rate, you'll hit $1,068 by month-end — almost DOUBLE your goal. What's happening here?"

Deep dive into spending patterns: $342 on DoorDash (11 orders in 22 days, averaging $31/order), $287 on Target "quick trips" (6 visits for "just one thing" that turned into carts full), $218 on happy hours/bars. Pattern identified: You order DoorDash every Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday when you're exhausted after work. Target runs happen on Saturday/Sunday when you're bored.

## Final Output

Accountability report with blunt, honest feedback, spending breakdown by category with average order amounts, root cause analysis (DoorDash = too tired to cook, Target = boredom shopping), and an 8-day action plan to finish February under $950 total: meal prep this Sunday (prevents midweek DoorDash burnout), no Target unless you have a written list with 3+ specific items, plan 2 FREE activities for next weekend (hike, museum, friend's house). Includes daily text check-ins for accountability. **Jessica finished February at $931 — still over budget, but she saved $137 in the final 8 days and identified her spending triggers.** March goal: $700 (more realistic than $600).
