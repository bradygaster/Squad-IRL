# Dating Profile Optimizer — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Tyler (28, software engineer, loves hiking and craft beer) has been on Hinge for 6 months with only 2 matches. His profile says "I like hiking, beer, and hanging with friends." His photos: 3 bathroom selfies, 1 group photo where you can't tell which person is him, 1 photo with his ex awkwardly cropped out.

## What the Agents Did

First draft improved bio but kept it safe and generic. Revised version shows actual personality: "I'm the guy who will drive 2 hours for a good IPA and then complain about the drive the whole way back. Professional software engineer by day, amateur sourdough baker by night (emphasis on amateur — my starter has a 40% survival rate and I've named each one that's died). Looking for someone who can appreciate both a 6am sunrise hike AND a lazy Sunday spent entirely in pajamas watching true crime documentaries."

Photo strategy: Replace ALL selfies (immediate red flag), lead with you on a hiking trail with natural lighting (shows hobby + good candid), delete the blurry group photo, add one with your dog if you have one, one at a brewery with friends but where you're clearly identifiable, one doing something active or interesting.

## Final Output

Optimized profile with specific, personality-driven bio that gives conversation hooks, unique prompt answers ("Most controversial opinion: Pineapple on pizza is not just acceptable, it's GOOD and I'll die on this hill"), 6 new photo recommendations with specific guidance on lighting/setting/what to wear, and conversation starter tips. **Tyler got 14 matches in the first week with his new profile.** He's now 3 months into dating someone he met on the app who also thinks pineapple pizza is underrated. They're planning a hiking trip together.
