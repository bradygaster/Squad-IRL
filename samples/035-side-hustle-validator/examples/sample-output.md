## Scenario

Keisha Morgan, 29, works full-time as an administrative assistant in Charlotte, North Carolina, earning $42,000/year. She loves dogs and wants to start a dog walking side hustle. She has evenings and weekends free, owns a reliable car, and has $500 in startup capital. She lives in the Myers Park neighborhood, which is affluent and full of dog owners. She wants to know: Is this viable? How much can she realistically earn? And what does she need to get started?

## 1. Market Analysis: Charlotte Dog Walking

### Market Size
- Charlotte metro has approximately 298,000 dog-owning households (American Veterinary Medical Association data, adjusted for Mecklenburg County)
- Myers Park (28207 zip code): estimated 4,200 households, ~38% dog ownership = **~1,600 dog-owning households**
- Adjacent walkable neighborhoods (Dilworth, Eastover, Elizabeth): additional ~3,800 dog-owning households
- **Total addressable market in Keisha's driving radius: ~5,400 dog-owning households**

### Competition
| Competitor | Type | Price Range | Rating | Weakness |
|-----------|------|-------------|--------|----------|
| Rover (platform) | App | $15–25/walk | 4.3★ | High service fees (20%), inconsistent quality |
| Wag (platform) | App | $14–22/walk | 3.8★ | Impersonal, high walker turnover |
| Charlotte Canine Care | Independent | $20–30/walk | 4.8★ | Waitlist — not accepting new clients |
| Bark & Stroll CLT | Independent | $18–25/walk | 4.6★ | Only covers South Charlotte, not Myers Park |
| Pawsitive Steps | Independent | $22–28/walk | 4.7★ | Only weekday daytime walks |
| 3 Solo Walkers (Rover/Nextdoor) | Independent | $15–20/walk | Mixed | Unreliable scheduling, no insurance |

**Key Finding:** Charlotte Canine Care has a *waitlist*. This means demand exceeds supply in the premium segment. Pawsitive Steps only offers daytime walks — Keisha's evening/weekend availability fills a gap nobody else covers.

### Competitive Advantage
1. **Evening and weekend availability** — most walkers (and all daytime services) stop at 5 PM
2. **Myers Park location** — affluent neighborhood with high willingness to pay
3. **Personal, consistent service** — not a rotating cast of app-assigned strangers
4. **Gap in market** — no established walker offers evening-only premium service in Myers Park

## 2. Pricing Strategy

### Recommended Pricing

| Service | Duration | Price | Basis |
|---------|----------|-------|-------|
| Standard Walk | 30 min | $22 | Market avg is $18–25; Myers Park premium justified |
| Extended Walk | 60 min | $35 | For high-energy dogs or multiple-dog households |
| Weekend Adventure Walk | 90 min (park/trail) | $45 | Premium experience — Freedom Park, McAlpine Creek |
| Drop-in Visit | 20 min | $18 | Quick potty break + feeding |
| Holiday/Weekend Premium | — | +$5/service | Standard for the industry |

### Why $22 for Standard Walk
- Below the premium competitor ($28 at Pawsitive Steps) → attractive pricing
- Above Rover average ($18) → signals quality, attracts clients who distrust cheap services
- At $22/walk, you only need 10 walks/week to clear $880/month — realistic for evening/weekend hours

But keep the day job until side hustle income sustains for 6+ consecutive months.
