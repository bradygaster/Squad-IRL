# Vet Appointment Prep — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Sarah Chen is preparing for a vet appointment for her 7-year-old golden retriever, **Biscuit** (neutered male, 78 lbs). Over the past two weeks, Biscuit has been limping intermittently on his front-right leg. The limping is worse after walks and first thing in the morning. Sarah has also noticed he's been eating about half his normal food portion and seems less enthusiastic about his daily fetch sessions in the backyard. Biscuit is currently on monthly heartworm prevention (Heartgard Plus) and a fish oil supplement. His last vet visit was 10 months ago for his annual wellness exam, where everything was normal. The appointment is scheduled for Thursday, March 13, 2025 at 2:30 PM with Dr. Kapoor at Riverside Animal Hospital.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**PetAdvocate** produced:

A two-page vet visit prep document covering Biscuit's limping and appetite changes. The document included a general summary paragraph stating that Biscuit "sometimes limps on his front leg" and "hasn't been eating as much lately." It listed five generic questions to ask the vet such as "What could be causing the limp?" and "Should we change his food?" The document mentioned Biscuit's age and breed but did not include weight, current medications, or vaccination history. There was a brief section on behavior changes noting he was "less playful" without specifics.

**SymptomLogger** scored it **65/100** and found:

- **Vague symptom timeline**: "Sometimes limps" and "past couple of weeks" give the vet nothing to work with — need specific dates, times of day, duration, and which leg with anatomical detail
- **No objective measurements**: Missing food intake quantities (cups per meal before vs. now), activity duration changes, weight if recently measured, and any temperature readings
- **Medication list incomplete**: Fish oil supplement is mentioned in passing but no dosage; heartworm prevention brand and dose not listed; no mention of any OTC pain meds Sarah may have tried
- **Questions are too generic**: "What could cause the limp?" is a conversation-starter, not a prepared question — need targeted questions organized by diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis
- **Missing trigger analysis**: No documentation of what makes the limp better or worse — surfaces (hardwood vs. grass), weather, time of day, activity level, rest duration
- **No photo or video documentation plan**: Intermittent limping may not present during the exam — Sarah needs video evidence and the document doesn't mention this

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**QuestionGenerator** addressed the feedback:

The document was restructured into a chronological symptom journal with specific dates, times, and observations. Objective measurements were added including food intake in cups, walk distances, and activity durations. A complete medication and supplement list with dosages was included. The question list was expanded to 24 targeted questions organized by category (diagnosis, treatment options, pain management, prognosis, lifestyle changes, and cost). A trigger analysis section was added documenting surfaces, weather, and activities correlated with limping episodes. Photo and video reminders were included with specific guidance on what to capture.

**SymptomLogger** scored it **93/100**: "Meets quality threshold."

## Trigger & Pattern Analysis

### What Makes the Limp WORSE
- **Morning / after rest**: Stiffness most pronounced after sleeping or lying down for 1+ hours
- **Cold or damp weather**: Worse on days below 45°F or after rain (Feb 22, Mar 1, Mar 8)
- **High-impact activity**: Running, fetch, sharp turns, playing with other dogs
- **Step-downs**: Porches, curbs, getting out of the car (6-18 inch drops)
- **Hard surfaces**: More noticeable on hardwood floors and concrete than on carpet or grass

### What Makes the Limp BETTER
- **Warming up**: Stiffness typically resolves after 15-25 minutes of gentle movement
- **Warm weather**: Better on mild afternoons (60°F+)
- **Flat, gentle walks**: Short (10-15 min) leash walks on flat sidewalk don't seem to aggravate
- **Soft surfaces**: Less noticeable on carpet and grass

### Pattern Summary
> Classic stiffness-after-rest pattern with activity-related flare-ups. Progressive over 2 weeks — morning stiffness duration increasing (5 min → 25 min), new stair avoidance, and appetite decline suggest worsening. No single acute injury event recalled.

## Prioritized Questions for Dr. Kapoor

### Category 1: Diagnosis & Assessment (Ask First)

1. Based on the symptom pattern — morning stiffness, activity-related flare-ups, progressive over 2 weeks — what are the most likely diagnoses you're considering?
2. Given Biscuit's age (7), breed (Golden Retriever), and the front-right leg involvement, how concerned are you about elbow dysplasia or osteoarthritis?
3. Is the decreased appetite likely related to pain from the limb issue, or should we investigate that as a separate concern?
4. Would you recommend X-rays today? If so, of which joints — just the right front leg, or both front legs for comparison?
5. Are there signs that would indicate a soft tissue injury (ligament, tendon) versus a joint/bone issue?
6. Could this be related to Lyme disease or another tick-borne illness? Biscuit has had tick exposure at Cedar Park. When was his last tick panel?
7. The increased licking of the right front leg — is that likely pain-directed grooming, or could there be a separate skin/paw issue?
8. Should we be concerned about referred pain — could a neck or shoulder issue be causing the front leg lameness?

### Category 2: Treatment Options

9. If this is early arthritis, what is the first-line treatment approach you'd recommend?
10. Are there joint supplements (glucosamine, chondroitin, MSM) you'd recommend, and at what dosages for a 78-lb dog?
11. Would you prescribe an NSAID for pain management? If so, which one, and what monitoring (bloodwork, etc.) would be needed?
12. Is physical therapy or rehabilitation an option? Can you refer us to a canine rehabilitation specialist in the area?
13. What about Adequan (polysulfated glycosaminoglycan) injections — would Biscuit be a candidate?
14. Should we consider a therapeutic diet (e.g., joint-support formula) given the appetite decrease?
15. Are there any complementary therapies (laser therapy, acupuncture, hydrotherapy) that have evidence for this condition?

### Category 3: Home Management & Lifestyle

16. What activity modifications should we make? Should we stop fetch entirely, or just limit it?
17. Should we switch Biscuit's sleeping arrangement? Would an orthopedic bed help? Any specific brands you recommend?
18. Are ramps for the car and porch steps worth investing in now?
19. What's the ideal exercise routine — duration, frequency, surface type — while we're managing this?
20. Should we adjust his food type or feeding schedule given the reduced appetite? Would warming the food or adding a topper help?
21. Is his current fish oil supplement (Nordic Naturals, 2200 mg/day) at a therapeutic dose for joint inflammation, or should we increase?

### Category 4: Prognosis & Long-Term Planning

22. If this is osteoarthritis, what does the typical progression look like for a Golden Retriever? What should we expect over the next 1-2 years?
23. Are there any breed-specific concerns (Golden Retrievers and cancer risk) that make this symptom pattern more urgent to investigate?
24. How often should we schedule follow-up visits to monitor this condition?
25. At what point would you recommend seeing a veterinary orthopedic specialist?
26. Is there a weight target we should aim for to reduce joint stress? Would losing 3-5 lbs help?

### Category 5: Cost & Logistics

27. What is the estimated cost for X-rays today if needed? (Sarah's budget note: Healthy Paws covers 80% after $250 deductible, which has been met for this year)
28. If bloodwork is recommended, what panels and approximate cost?
29. If we end up needing an orthopedic specialist referral, what's the typical cost range for an initial consultation and advanced imaging (CT/MRI)?
30. Are there any generic alternatives to brand-name medications that would be equally effective?
31. For supplements, are veterinary-branded products significantly better than quality human-grade supplements at appropriate doses?

- [ ] Ask about side effects to watch for with any new medications
- [ ] Schedule follow-up appointment (ask recommended timeframe)
- [ ] Get Dr. Kapoor's direct line or email for questions that come up at home
- [ ] Ask: "Is there anything else I should be monitoring or documenting before our next visit?"

### At-Home Monitoring Plan (Start After Visit)
- [ ] Daily limp log: Time of day, severity (1-10), duration, activity context
- [ ] Daily food intake tracking: Cups consumed AM/PM
- [ ] Weekly weigh-in (pet store scale or vet tech walk-in)
- [ ] Weekly video: 30-second clip of Biscuit walking on flat surface for gait comparison
- [ ] Note any new symptoms: vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy beyond current baseline, new areas of pain
- [ ] Track medication administration and any side effects

### Red Flags — Call the Vet Immediately If:
- 🚨 Biscuit stops bearing weight on the right front leg entirely
- 🚨 Sudden swelling, heat, or visible deformity in the leg
- 🚨 Stops eating for 24+ hours or stops drinking water
- 🚨 Vomiting or diarrhea (especially if starting a new NSAID)
- 🚨 Sudden behavior change: hiding, excessive panting, aggression
- 🚨 Any new lameness in other legs
