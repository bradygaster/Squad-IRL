**Layout Logic:**
- **Tallest plants (tomatoes) in the back** so they don't shade shorter plants
- **Trellis for cucumbers** saves 8+ sq ft of ground space and improves air circulation

- **Marigold border** — French marigolds repel aphids, whiteflies, and nematodes
- **Rosemary in the corner** — it's a perennial that will grow for years; give it permanent space

## 4. Week-by-Week Planting Calendar

### Indoor Seed Starting (Optional — or buy transplants)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Feb 15–22** | Start tomato seeds indoors (8 weeks before transplant) |
| **Feb 22–Mar 1** | Start pepper seeds indoors (8–10 weeks before transplant) |
| **Mar 15** | Start cucumber and zucchini seeds indoors (4 weeks before transplant) |
| **Mar 15** | Start basil seeds indoors (6 weeks before transplant) |

**Beginner tip:** Skip seed starting the first year. Buy transplants from a local nursery (Logan's Garden Shop on Hillsborough St. or the NC State Farmers Market). It's easier, faster, and the plants are already hardened off.

### Outdoor Calendar

| Week | Date Range | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Week 1** | Mar 15–21 | **Soil prep.** Add 2–3" of compost to raised bed. Mix in. Water deeply. Let sit 1 week. |
| **Week 2** | Mar 22–28 | **Plant cilantro and parsley** — these are cool-season herbs that can handle light frost. Direct sow seeds ½" deep. |
| **Week 3** | Mar 29–Apr 4 | **Plant rosemary** (transplant from nursery). Water in well. |
| **Week 4** | Apr 5–11 | **Watch for last frost** (avg April 11). Have frost cloth ready ($8 at Lowe's) just in case. |
| **Week 5** | Apr 12–18 | **🟢 GO WEEK: Plant tomatoes, peppers, and basil.** Soil temp should be 60°F+. Dig hole, add handful of compost, set transplant, water deeply. Install tomato cages immediately. |
| **Week 6** | Apr 19–25 | **Mulch everything** — 2–3" of straw or shredded leaves around all plants. This retains moisture, suppresses weeds, and regulates soil temperature. |
| **Week 7** | Apr 26–May 2 | **Install cucumber trellis.** A 4-ft tall wire mesh or cattle panel works great. |
| **Week 8** | May 3–9 | **Plant cucumbers and zucchini.** Soil temp should be 70°F+. Direct sow 2 seeds per spot, thin to 1 after germination. Or transplant seedlings. |
| **Week 9** | May 10–16 | **Plant marigold border.** Buy a flat of French Dwarf marigolds from any garden center (~$4/flat). Space 8" apart. |
| **Week 10** | May 17–23 | **First fertilizer application.** Use a balanced organic fertilizer (Espoma Garden-tone, $12 at Lowe's). Follow package directions. |
| **Week 11–14** | May 24–Jun 20 | **Maintenance mode.** Water, weed, watch. Side-dress tomatoes with fertilizer when first flowers appear. |
| **Week 15+** | Jun 21+ | **🎉 HARVEST BEGINS.** Cucumbers and herbs first, then zucchini, then tomatoes and peppers in July. |

## 5. Watering Guide

### The Golden Rules
1. **Water the soil, not the leaves** — wet leaves in NC humidity = fungal disease
2. **Water deeply but less often** — 1" of water, 2–3 times per week beats a daily sprinkle
3. **Water in the morning** (before 10 AM) — gives leaves time to dry before evening humidity
4. **Check soil moisture** — stick your finger 2" into the soil. Dry? Water. Moist? Skip.

### Watering Schedule

| Month | Frequency | Amount per Session | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| April | 2× per week | 1" (about 10 gal for 160 sq ft bed) | Cooler temps, less evaporation |
| May | 2–3× per week | 1" | Increasing heat |
| June | 3× per week | 1–1.5" | Heat ramps up; mulch helps |
| July–Aug | Every other day | 1.5" | Peak heat; may need daily in 95°F+ weeks |
| September | 2–3× per week | 1" | Gradually taper |

**Pro tip:** Install a drip irrigation kit ($25–40 at Home Depot) or a soaker hose ($15). Set on a timer ($20). Total investment: $35–60. Saves you 20 minutes per watering session and delivers water directly to roots.

## 6. Common Raleigh Pests & Diseases

| Problem | What to Look For | Organic Solution | Prevention |
|---------|------------------|------------------|------------|
| **Tomato Hornworm** | Large green caterpillar, 3–4"; chewed leaves | Hand-pick and relocate (or feed to chickens). BT spray (Bacillus thuringiensis) | Inspect plants 2× weekly |
| **Squash Vine Borer** | Wilting zucchini despite adequate water; sawdust-like frass at stem base | Slit stem carefully, remove larva, bury stem in soil to re-root | Wrap stem base with aluminum foil in June |
| **Aphids** | Tiny green/black clusters on leaf undersides; sticky residue | Strong water spray from hose; or neem oil spray | Marigold border (already in your plan!) |
| **Early Blight (tomatoes)** | Brown spots with concentric rings on lower leaves | Remove affected leaves; copper fungicide spray | Mulch to prevent soil splash; water at base only |
| **Powdery Mildew** | White powdery coating on squash/cucumber leaves (common in NC humidity) | Spray with 1 tbsp baking soda + 1 gal water + drop of dish soap | Good air circulation; trellis cucumbers (already planned!) |
| **Blossom End Rot** | Black sunken spot on bottom of tomatoes/peppers | Not a pest — calcium deficiency from inconsistent watering | Water consistently; don't let soil dry out then flood |
