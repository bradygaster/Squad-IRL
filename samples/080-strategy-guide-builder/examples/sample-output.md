## 1. Core Mechanics

### Energy

You start each day with **270 energy**. Almost every useful action costs energy:

| Action | Energy Cost |
|---|---|
| Watering one tile (watering can) | 2 |
| Hoeing one tile | 2 |
| Chopping a tree (axe) | 2 |
| Breaking a rock (pickaxe) | 2 |
| Fishing (per cast) | 8 |
| Mining a rock (in the mines) | 2–4 |

**Recovery:** Eating food restores energy. Early game, foraged items (Spring Onion, Leek, Daffodil) are your main source. Sleeping restores energy to full but sleeping after midnight incurs a penalty (you wake with less energy).

**Key rule:** Never waste energy on actions you don't need. Don't water empty tiles. Don't clear farm debris you don't need cleared yet. Plan your energy budget for each day.

### Time and Seasons

- **1 day** = 7:00 AM to 2:00 AM (you pass out and lose money if you stay up past 2 AM)
- **1 season** = 28 days
- **4 seasons** per year: Spring → Summer → Fall → Winter
- **Crops die when a season changes** (unless they are multi-season crops). You must replant each season.
- **10 minutes** of game time ≈ 7 seconds of real time

### Skills

There are 5 skill categories. You level them up by doing the relevant activity:

| Skill | Leveled By | Key Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Farming | Harvesting crops | Sprinklers (Lv 2, 6, 9), Quality crops |
| Mining | Breaking rocks | Bombs (crafting), better pickaxe efficiency |
| Foraging | Picking wild items, chopping trees | Tree seeds, better axe efficiency, Tracker |
| Fishing | Catching fish | Better rods, Trap bobber, more tackle |
| Combat | Killing monsters in the mines | Better rings, defense buffs |

At levels 5 and 10 of each skill, you choose a **profession** that gives a permanent bonus. Choose wisely — you can change later, but it costs 10,000g.

### Relationships

- Talk to NPCs daily for +20 friendship points
- Give them 2 gifts per week for +80 (liked) or +160 (loved) points per gift
- Each heart = 250 points, max 8 hearts (10 with a bouquet for datable NPCs, 14 if married)
- Birthday gifts give **8× normal points** — always mark birthdays on your calendar

## 2. Spring Year 1 — Getting Started

### Day-by-Day: Your First Week

#### Day 1 (Monday, Spring 1)

**Morning:**
1. Watch the intro cutscene. You wake up in your farmhouse.
2. Open your inventory: you have 15 Parsnip Seeds, a Hoe, Watering Can, Axe, Pickaxe, and Scythe.
3. Go outside. Use your Hoe to till 15 tiles near the pond on your farm (stay close to water).
4. Plant all 15 Parsnip Seeds. Water them all. (~30 energy for hoeing, ~30 for watering = 60 energy used)

**Afternoon:**
5. Clear some debris on the farm (sticks and rocks) to start gathering wood and stone. Aim for 50 wood today.
6. Walk to town (south from your farm). Visit Pierre's General Store. Buy as many Potato Seeds as you can afford with your remaining 500g (they cost 50g each = 10 seeds).
7. Introduce yourself to any NPCs you pass. Talk to everyone — you need to start building friendships.

**Evening:**
8. Go home. Plant and water your Potato Seeds if you have energy.
9. Go to bed before midnight. You need full energy tomorrow.

**Day 1 Targets:** 15 Parsnips planted + 10 Potatoes planted. 50+ wood collected.

#### Day 2 (Tuesday, Spring 2)

1. **Water all crops first thing** — this is your daily ritual every morning, no exceptions.
2. Forage the farm and surrounding areas for Spring wild items: Leek, Daffodil, Dandelion, Spring Onion (south of your farm, across the bridge).
3. Visit the Beach — you'll find an old bamboo fishing rod from Willy. **Take it.** Fishing is your #1 money maker in early Spring.
4. Spend the rest of the day fishing in the mountain lake (north of Robin's house) or the ocean. Keep everything you catch.
5. Sell fish in the shipping bin before bed. Target: earn 300–500g today from fishing.

#### Day 3 (Wednesday, Spring 3)

1. Water crops.
2. Check the mail — you may have gotten a letter from Willy.
3. Spend most of the day fishing. You should be improving at the minigame by now.
4. If you've earned enough, buy more seeds from Pierre: Potatoes (50g) are the best Spring crop for money.
5. Goal: have at least 200g saved after buying seeds.

#### Day 4 (Thursday, Spring 4)

1. Water crops. By now you're feeling the energy crunch — eat foraged items to extend your day.
2. **Visit the mines** (unlocked on Day 5, but if you've gotten the letter, go explore). If not open yet, fish more.
3. Plant any new seeds you bought.
4. Talk to 3–5 NPCs in town.

#### Day 5 (Friday, Spring 5)

1. Water crops.
2. **The Mines open today** (Joja cutscene in the mountains). Visit floor 1–5.
3. Your goal in the mines: collect 20 Copper Ore and start working toward your first Furnace (requires 20 Copper Ore + 25 Stone — you'll get the crafting recipe from Clint after finding copper).
4. Don't go below floor 5 yet — your weapon is weak and you'll take unnecessary damage.

#### Days 6–7 (Weekend)

1. Water crops every morning (always!).
2. Split time between fishing (money) and mines (resources).
3. Build a Chest (50 wood) and place it near your crops. Start organizing: one chest for crops/seeds, one for ores/bars.
4. **Sunday tip:** Check the Traveling Cart in Cindersap Forest (south of your farm, Friday and Sunday 6AM–8PM). She sometimes sells rare items, seeds for future seasons, or Coffee Beans early.

### Spring Goals Checklist

- [ ] Plant and harvest Parsnips (4 days), Potatoes (6 days), Cauliflower (12 days)
- [ ] Reach Farming Level 2 (unlocks Quality Sprinkler recipe at Lv 6 — long way off, but every harvest counts)
- [ ] Reach Mine Floor 40 by end of Spring (for Iron Ore access)
- [ ] Build a Silo (100 stone, 10 clay, 5 copper bars — from Robin) before clearing all grass
- [ ] Upgrade Watering Can to Copper (2,000g + 5 Copper Bars at Clint's — **do this on a day before rain** so you don't miss a watering day)
- [ ] Catch the Legend fish? No — save this goal for Year 2. Focus on Cat Fish and other Spring catches for income
- [ ] Earn at least 10,000g total by end of Spring
- [ ] Start the Community Center bundles (visit the Community Center after Spring 5, watch the Junimo cutscene)

★ **Best beginner crop.** Blueberries are the safest Summer investment: they produce 3 berries per harvest, regrow, and have high total yield. Buy as many as you can afford on Summer 1.

### Summer Strategy

1. **Summer 1:** Buy Blueberry and Melon seeds with all your money. Plant immediately. If you can afford Starfruit from the Oasis (Desert), buy some — but don't sweat it Year 1.
2. **Sprinklers are your priority.** If you've reached Farming Lv 2, craft basic Sprinklers (1 Copper Bar + 1 Iron Bar). They water 4 adjacent tiles. Not amazing, but they save energy.
3. **Push mines to Floor 80** for Gold Ore. You need Gold Bars for Quality Sprinklers (Farming Lv 6: 1 Gold Bar + 1 Iron Bar + 1 Refined Quartz).
4. **Build a Coop** from Robin (4,000g, 300 wood, 100 stone) — buy a Chicken, start producing Eggs for the Animal Bundle and income.
5. **Summer foraging** on the beach — Coral, Sea Urchin, and Rainbow Shell are valuable.

### Fall Strategy

1. **Plant Cranberries and Pumpkins on Fall 1.** If you find a Rare Seed from the Traveling Cart (1,000g), plant it — it must be planted by Fall 2 to mature by Fall 26.

## 5. Winter Year 1 — Regroup and Prepare

Winter is the **planning and preparation** season. No crops grow outdoors (except Winter Seeds from foraging).

### Winter Strategy

1. **Mine, mine, mine.** With no crops to water, all your energy goes to the mines. Reach Floor 120 if you haven't already.
2. **Upgrade tools to Gold or Iridium.** No crops means no penalty for dropping off your Watering Can for 2 days.
3. **Fish for money.** Winter fish are valuable: Sturgeon (200g), Lingcod (120g), and Squid (night, 80g) are good earners.
4. **Craft and place sprinklers.** Build 30–40 Quality Sprinklers so you're ready for Spring Year 2 with zero manual watering.
5. **Redesign your farm layout.** Clear debris, plan crop plots, organize chests, build paths.
6. **Build the Kitchen upgrade** (10,000g + 450 wood from Robin). Cooking restores energy and gives buffs.
7. **Farm Winter Seeds.** Forage Crystal Fruit, Snow Yam, Winter Root, and Crocus → craft into Winter Seeds → plant → more foraging items → more seeds. Repeat. This levels up Foraging skill and gives some income.

### Year 1 Progress Checklist

By the end of Year 1, you should have:

- [ ] 30,000–50,000g in savings
- [ ] Farming Level 6+ (Quality Sprinklers unlocked)
- [ ] Mines completed to Floor 120 (Skull Key obtained)
- [ ] At least 1 Coop animal and 1 Barn animal
- [ ] Copper/Steel tools minimum, ideally Gold
- [ ] 20+ Quality Sprinklers placed
- [ ] 3+ hearts with at least 5 NPCs
- [ ] 50%+ of Community Center bundles complete

## 6. Year 2 Goals and Advanced Strategies

### Unlock Priorities

| Unlock | Requirement | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Skull Cavern | Finish Mines Floor 120, get Skull Key | Iridium Ore for best sprinklers/tools |
| Greenhouse | Complete Pantry bundles | Grow any crop year-round, including Starfruit and Ancient Fruit |
| Desert access | Complete Vault bundle (42,500g) | Skull Cavern, Sandy's shop, Starfruit Seeds |
| Iridium Sprinklers | Farming Lv 9 + Iridium Bars | Water 24 tiles each — eliminates watering entirely |

### Ancient Fruit Strategy (End-Game Money Machine)

1. Find an Ancient Seed (artifact) → donate to Gunther at the Museum → receive Ancient Seeds packet + crafting recipe
2. Plant Ancient Seeds in the Greenhouse → takes 28 days to first harvest, then regrows every 7 days forever
3. Put harvested Ancient Fruit into Kegs (30 Oak Resin + 1 Iron Bar + 1 Copper Bar each)
4. Ancient Fruit Wine sells for **1,650g** base (2,310g with Artisan profession)
5. Fill the Greenhouse with Ancient Fruit + Kegs nearby = **100,000g+ per month on autopilot**

### Skull Cavern Tips

- Bring 20+ Bombs (Iron Ore × 4 + Coal × 1) and 20+ Mega Bombs
- Bring a stack of food: Cheese, Spicy Eel (luck + speed buff), or Pumpkin Soup
- Use staircases (99 Stone each) to skip floors with no iridium
- Go on **high luck days** (check TV fortune teller each morning)
- Target: reach Floor 100+ for maximum Iridium Ore density

## 8. Mine Progression Guide

### Floor Breakdown

| Floors | Ore Available | Enemies | Recommended Gear | Tips |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–39 | Copper, Geodes | Slimes, Bugs | Rusty Sword → pick up better drops | Kill every Slime for early Combat XP |
| 40–79 | Iron, Frozen Geodes | Dust Sprites, Frost Bats | Steel Smallsword, Leather Boots | Dust Sprites drop Coal — farm floors 41–49 for Coal |
| 80–119 | Gold, Magma Geodes | Shadow Brutes, Squid Kids | Obsidian Edge, Space Boots | Bring 30+ food items, these enemies hit hard |
| 120 | Skull Key reward | — | — | Congratulations! You've unlocked Skull Cavern |

### Equipment Progression

| Tier | Weapon | Damage | How to Get |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | Rusty Sword | 2–5 | Given at Mine Floor 1 |
| Early | Steel Smallsword | 4–8 | Drop or Adventurer's Guild (1,000g) |
| Mid | Obsidian Edge | 30–45 | Drop from Shadow Brutes (Floors 80+) or Guild (4,000g) |
| Late | Lava Katana | 55–64 | Adventurer's Guild (25,000g) |
| End-game | Galaxy Sword | 60–80 | Bring Prismatic Shard to desert pillars |

### Mine Tips

- **Elevator saves progress every 5 floors** — always push to the next elevator floor before leaving
- **Bring food:** Field Snacks (crafted: 1 Acorn + 1 Maple Seed + 1 Pine Cone), Cheese, or Sashimi (any fish in a kitchen)
- **Lucky days matter:** Check the Fortune Teller on TV. On lucky days, ladders appear faster and gem nodes are more common
- **Craft bombs** as soon as possible (4 Iron Ore + 1 Coal = 1 Bomb). They clear large areas of rock instantly — much faster than pickaxing
- **Always bring a weapon to the mines.** Sounds obvious, but many beginners forget after upgrading their pickaxe

## 11. Common Beginner Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Clearing Your Entire Farm on Day 1

You don't need all that space yet. You'll waste all your energy and have nothing left for planting. **Clear only what you need for crops.** The rest can wait.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Ignoring Fishing

Fishing is tedious to learn, but it's the best money-maker in Spring Year 1 when you have few crops. Even catching basic fish at the Mountain Lake nets 300–500g/day early on. Catfish on rainy days sells for 200g each.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Hoarding Everything

It's good to keep a few of each item for bundles, but new players often refuse to sell anything. **Sell your excess crops and fish.** You need gold to buy seeds, upgrade tools, and build structures. Check the Community Center bundles list and keep 1 of each needed item — sell the rest.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Staying Up Past Midnight

At 12 AM your character starts moving slower. At 2 AM you pass out and lose up to 1,000g. **Go to bed by midnight.** Losing gold is never worth squeezing in 10 more minutes.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Planting Crops on the Last Day of a Season

All crops (except multi-season crops like Coffee, Wheat, and Corn) **die when the season changes.** Don't plant Parsnips on Spring 26 — they take 4 days and will die on Summer 1. Check growth times before planting late.

### ❌ Mistake 6: Upgrading Your Watering Can Without Checking the Weather

It takes 2 days to upgrade your watering can. During that time, you can't water crops. **Always start the upgrade on the day BEFORE a rainy day** (check TV weather forecast). Water everything the morning you drop it off, then rain covers the next day, and you pick it up the day after.

### ❌ Mistake 7: Skipping the Community Center

The Joja route (buying a membership for 5,000g) is tempting because it's simpler, but the Community Center route gives you the **Greenhouse, Bus Repair, Minecarts, and Bridge Repair** — all hugely valuable. The bundles also force you to explore all aspects of the game.

## 12. Tips and Tricks

### Daily Routine Optimization

1. **Morning:** Water crops (skip if sprinklered) → check TV (weather, luck, recipes, tips) → harvest any ready crops
2. **Mid-day:** Main activity (mines, fishing, foraging, or town errands)
3. **Evening:** Process artisan goods, pet animals, talk to 2–3 NPCs, gift if possible
4. **Night:** Go home by 11 PM, organize chests, sleep

### Hidden Tricks Most Guides Don't Tell You

- **Hold right-click** to eat food — faster than going through inventory
- **Holding a tool button** charges it up. A charged watering can waters multiple tiles in a line (3 tiles at Copper, 5 at Steel, etc.)
- The **TV cooking show** teaches you 1 new recipe every Sunday and Wednesday. Watch it every time — recipes are one of the most missable items
- **Speed buffs stack** with food. Coffee (+1 speed) stacks with Spicy Eel (+1 speed) for +2 total, making you zoom around
- **Placing a chest at the Mine entrance** lets you swap items between trips without going home
- **Tappers on Oak Trees** give Oak Resin every 7 days — you'll need hundreds for Kegs later. Start tapping Year 1.
- **Crab Pots in your farm pond** catch Snails, Crayfish, and Periwinkle for free every day. Use them for Sashimi.
- Press **ESC to skip cutscenes** if you've seen them before (but watch them the first time — the writing is delightful)

## 13. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Artisan Goods** | Processed items made in Kegs, Preserve Jars, Oil Makers, etc. Worth significantly more than raw products |
| **Bundle** | A set of items you donate to the Community Center Junimos to earn rewards and unlock areas |
| **CC** | Community Center — the building in town where you complete bundles |
| **Energy** | Your stamina bar. Depleted by tools and actions; restored by food and sleeping |
| **Foraging** | Picking up items found in the wild (not planted by you). Levels up the Foraging skill |
| **Heart Event** | A cutscene that triggers when you reach a certain friendship level with an NPC |
| **Iridium** | The rarest ore, found primarily in Skull Cavern. Used for the best tools and sprinklers |
| **Keg** | Artisan equipment that turns fruits into Wine and vegetables into Juice. Essential for end-game profit |
| **Quality** | Items can be Normal, Silver ★, Gold ★★, or Iridium ★★★. Higher quality = higher sell price |
| **Regrow Crop** | A crop that produces again after its first harvest without replanting (e.g., Blueberry, Cranberry, Hops) |
| **ROI** | Return on Investment — profit divided by cost. Higher ROI = better value per gold spent on seeds |
| **Skull Cavern** | An infinite-depth mine in the Desert. Source of Iridium Ore and rare loot. Unlocked after completing the regular Mines |
| **Sprinkler** | Placed equipment that automatically waters nearby crops every morning. Three tiers: Basic (4 tiles), Quality (8 tiles), Iridium (24 tiles) |
| **Tapper** | A craftable item placed on trees to extract Maple Syrup, Oak Resin, or Pine Tar over time |
| **XP** | Experience points earned for skill activities (harvesting, fishing, mining, etc.). Fill the bar to level up and unlock new recipes |
