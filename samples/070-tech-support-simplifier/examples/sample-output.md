## Scenario

Margaret, 68, just got a new iPhone 15 from her daughter for her birthday. She's used a flip phone for 15 years. Her daughter lives across the country and sent Margaret a link to Apple's setup guide, which says things like "Navigate to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Prepare for New iPhone." Margaret doesn't know what "Navigate to Settings" means, can't find the "General" button, and is on the verge of tears thinking she's too old for this. She needs help setting up her email that won't make her feel stupid.

# HOW TO SET UP EMAIL ON YOUR NEW iPHONE
**Step-by-Step Guide for Margaret**

---

## BEFORE YOU START: What You'll Need

✓ Your iPhone (fully charged or plugged in)  
✓ Your email address (example: margaret@gmail.com)  
✓ Your email password (the password you use to check email on your computer)  
✓ About 10-15 minutes of quiet time  
✓ Reading glasses if you need them

**Don't worry if this feels overwhelming!** We'll go very slowly, one step at a time. You can't break anything by following these instructions.

---

## STEP 1: Unlock Your iPhone

**What to do:**
1. Pick up your iPhone
2. Look at the screen — if it's black, press the button on the RIGHT side of the phone (about halfway up the edge)
3. The screen will light up and show you the time

**Next:**
4. Swipe your finger UP from the very bottom of the screen to the top (like you're brushing crumbs off a table)
5. If it asks for a passcode, type in the numbers your daughter set up with you

**You should see:** Lots of colorful square icons (these are called "apps")

**If that didn't work:** Press the side button again and try the swipe motion once more, a bit faster this time.

---

## STEP 2: Find the Settings App

**What to do:**
1. Look at your screen carefully
2. Find the icon that looks like **gray gears** (like the gears inside a watch)
3. Under the gray gears, it says "Settings"

**Here's what it looks like:**  
[The Settings icon is gray with two interlocking gears, positioned usually on the first page of apps]

**Found it?** Great! Move to Step 3.

**Can't find it?**  
- Swipe your finger from right to left across the screen (this shows you more apps)
- Look again for the gray gears icon
- It's usually on the first or second page of apps
- Still can't find it? Ask Siri: Hold down the button on the right side and say "Open Settings"

---

## STEP 3: Open Settings

**What to do:**
1. Tap your finger ONCE on the gray gears icon (Settings)
2. Take your finger OFF quickly (don't hold it down, just a quick tap)

**You should see:** A new screen appears with a list of options. At the very top, you might see your name.

**If nothing happened:** You might have held your finger down too long. Try again with a quicker tap.

---

## STEP 4: Scroll Down to Find "Mail"

**What to do:**
1. Put your finger in the middle of the screen
2. Slide your finger UP the screen slowly (this makes the list scroll down — yes, that's backwards, but that's how it works!)
3. Keep scrolling until you see the word **"Mail"** with a blue envelope icon next to it

**You should see:** A list of words like "Notifications," "Sounds," "Focus," and eventually "Mail"

**Tip:** The "Mail" option is usually about halfway down the list, after "Phone" and before "Contacts"

**Found "Mail"?** Perfect! Move to Step 5.

---

## STEP 5: Open Mail Settings

**What to do:**
1. Tap ONCE on the word "Mail" (the one with the blue envelope icon)

**You should see:** A new screen that says "Mail" at the top

---

## STEP 6: Tap "Accounts"

**What to do:**
1. Look for the word **"Accounts"** in blue letters
2. It's usually the first option at the top
3. Tap ONCE on "Accounts"

**You should see:** A new screen that says "Accounts" at the top, with a blue button that says "Add Account"

---

## STEP 7: Tap "Add Account"

**What to do:**
1. Look for the blue button that says **"Add Account"**
2. Tap ONCE on it

**You should see:** A list of email providers (companies) with their logos:
- Google (colorful letters)
- Yahoo! (purple)
- Outlook (blue)
- And others

---

## STEP 8: Choose Your Email Provider

**What to do:**
Now you need to know which company provides your email. Look at your email address:

**If your email ends in @gmail.com:**
- Tap on **"Google"** (the one with colorful letters)

**If your email ends in @yahoo.com:**
- Tap on **"Yahoo"** (the purple one)

**If your email ends in @outlook.com, @hotmail.com, or @live.com:**
- Tap on **"Outlook.com"** (the blue one)

**If you have a different email (like @aol.com, @icloud.com, or something else):**
- Tap on **"Other"** at the bottom of the list

**Not sure which email you have?**
- Check an old email on your computer or ask your daughter
- Or look at your email address written down — the part after the @ tells you

---

## STEP 9: Type Your Email Address

**What to do:**
1. The screen will show a box that says "Email"
2. Tap ONCE in that box
3. A keyboard will appear at the bottom of the screen
4. Type your FULL email address (example: margaret@gmail.com)
   - Include the @ symbol (find it on the keyboard)
   - Include everything after the @
   - Check for typos!

**You should see:** Your email address in the box

**Important:** Type carefully! Even one wrong letter means it won't work.

**After typing:**
5. Tap the blue button that says **"Next"** (it's at the top right corner of the screen)

---

## STEP 10: Type Your Password

**What to do:**
1. The screen will now show a box that says "Password"
2. Tap ONCE in that box
3. Type your email password (the one you use on your computer)
4. You'll see dots instead of letters as you type — that's normal! It's hiding your password for security.

**Important tips:**
- Type carefully — passwords are case-sensitive (capital letters matter!)
- If you're not sure of your password, try the one you wrote down
- Don't have your password? You'll need to reset it on your computer first (ask your daughter for help with that)

**After typing:**
5. Tap the blue button that says **"Next"** at the top right

**What happens next:** The phone will try to connect to your email. This takes 10-30 seconds. You'll see a spinning circle.

---

## STEP 11: Wait for It to Connect

**What to do:**
1. Be patient — don't tap anything!
2. Watch for the spinning circle to stop

**You should see ONE of these things:**

**SUCCESS:** A new screen appears asking what you want to sync (Mail, Contacts, Calendars, Notes). Everything will have green switches next to it.
- If you see this, **move to Step 12** — you're almost done!

**ERROR:** A message pops up saying "Cannot Get Mail" or "Incorrect password"
- If you see this, **go to the Troubleshooting section below**

---

## STEP 12: Choose What to Sync (Almost Done!)

**What to do:**
1. You'll see several options with green switches:
   - **Mail** (keep this ON — green)
   - **Contacts** (your choice — green means yes, gray means no)
   - **Calendars** (your choice)
   - **Notes** (your choice)

**My recommendation:** Keep Mail ON (green), and you can turn the others ON too if you want your contacts and calendar on your phone.

**To turn something ON or OFF:**
- Tap the little switch once — it slides from green (on) to gray (off) or vice versa

**After choosing:**
2. Tap the blue "Save" button at the top right corner

**You should see:** The screen returns to "Accounts" and now your email (like "Gmail" or "Yahoo") is listed!

---

## STEP 13: Check That It Worked

**What to do:**
1. Press the button on the left side of your phone (near the top) to go back to the home screen
2. Look for the **Mail app** — it's a blue square icon with a white envelope
3. Tap on it ONCE

**You should see:** Your email inbox! You might see new emails loading.

**SUCCESS! You did it!** 🎉

---

## TROUBLESHOOTING: If Something Went Wrong

### Problem: "Cannot Get Mail" or "Incorrect Password"

**What this means:** Usually, your password was typed wrong OR your email provider needs extra security steps.

**What to do:**
1. Tap "OK" on the error message
2. Try typing your password again (tap in the password box, delete what's there, type it carefully again)
3. Tap "Next"

**Still not working?**
- Your email might need "2-factor authentication" or an "app password" — this is a security feature
- You'll need to create an "app-specific password" on your computer
- Ask your daughter to help with this part, or call Apple Support: 1-800-APL-CARE (they're very patient!)

---

### Problem: I Can't Find the Settings Icon

**What to do:**
1. Swipe down from the MIDDLE of your home screen (the screen with all the apps)
2. A search box appears at the top
3. Type "Settings"
4. Tap on the gray gears icon that appears

---

### Problem: I Tapped the Wrong Thing and Now I'm Lost

**What to do:**
1. Don't panic — you can always go back!
2. Look for a blue word at the TOP LEFT of the screen (usually says "Back" or has a < arrow)
3. Tap on it to go back one screen
4. Find your place in the instructions again and continue

**Super lost?**
- Press the button at the bottom middle of the phone (the "Home button") OR swipe up from the very bottom if you have a newer iPhone
- This takes you back to the home screen
- Start again from Step 2

---

### Problem: The Keyboard Disappeared

**What to do:**
1. Tap ONCE in the box where you're supposed to type (the email or password box)
2. The keyboard will pop back up

---

### Problem: I Made a Typo in My Email Address

**What to do:**
1. Tap in the email box where the typo is
2. Press the **Delete button** on the keyboard (looks like a ← with an X) to erase the wrong letters
3. Type the correct letters
4. Check again carefully!
5. Tap "Next"

---

## SAFETY TIPS ⚠️

**IMPORTANT — Please Read:**

1. **Never share your password with anyone who calls or texts you.** Apple will NEVER call and ask for your password. Scammers do this. It's always a scam.

2. **If someone texts saying "Your account is locked, click here"** — DON'T CLICK! It's a scam. Delete the text.

3. **If you're not sure if something is real:** Call your daughter or call Apple directly at 1-800-275-2273 (that's 1-800-APL-CARE). Don't use a phone number from a text or email.

4. **Your iPhone is secure.** You can't "break" it by tapping the wrong thing. If you get confused, you can always close the app and start over.
