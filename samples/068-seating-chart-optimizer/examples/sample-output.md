# Seating Chart Optimizer — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Rachel and Tom are getting married next month with 120 guests at their reception. They have 15 tables of 8 people each. The problem: Rachel's parents are divorced and both remarried (Dad's new wife Amanda is 15 years younger than Rachel — awkward), Tom's college fraternity brothers get rowdy when together, Rachel's boss expects VIP treatment, three single guests asked not to be "the lonely single at a couples table," Aunt Linda and Aunt Margaret haven't spoken in 6 years over inheritance drama, and Uncle Joe uses a wheelchair. Rachel is crying over the seating chart that she's redrawn 11 times.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**SeatingPlanner** produced:

*Basic Seating Chart - 15 Tables*

Table 1 (Head Table): Bride, Groom, Parents, Best Man, Maid of Honor
Table 2: Rachel's mom + stepdad, Tom's parents
Table 3: Rachel's dad + Amanda, other family
Table 4-15: Mixed guests

**Reviewer** scored it **61/100** and found:

- Putting divorced parents at different tables solves one problem but creates another: who sits at head table? Both moms will feel slighted if the other is chosen
- Rachel's dad's table with "other family" is vague and could accidentally seat him near Rachel's mom's side (drama)
- No consideration of Uncle Joe's wheelchair accessibility near restrooms/exits
- Rowdy fraternity brothers aren't separated — they'll get loud and disrupt nearby tables
- Single guests concern not addressed at all
- Aunt Linda and Aunt Margaret conflict not accounted for
- Rachel's boss placement not considered (VIP positioning)
- No explanation of *why* specific people are seated together
- Missing table layout map relative to dance floor, bar, bathrooms

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**ConflictResolver** addressed the feedback:

*Completely restructured seating strategy. Created modified head table with just bride, groom, best man, and maid of honor (smaller, eliminates parent conflict). Placed Rachel's mom's family at Table 2 (near head table, left side) and Rachel's dad's family at Table 3 (near head table, right side) — physically separated by head table barrier. Positioned Uncle Joe at Table 7 near accessible entrance and restroom corridor. Split fraternity brothers across Tables 12, 13, 14 with calming influences (their significant others, mature friends) at each table. Created "fabulous singles" Table 9 with interesting, social singles who'll have great conversations. Seated Aunt Linda at Table 5, Aunt Margaret at Table 11 (opposite sides of room). Placed Rachel's boss at Table 2 with Rachel's mom (appropriate VIP positioning). Added venue layout map showing table positions relative to key locations.*

**Reviewer** scored it **94/100**: "Excellent conflict management and thoughtful social dynamics."
