# Running Training Plan — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Marcus Chen, age 34, is an intermediate runner based in Denver, Colorado. He completed his first marathon (Denver Marathon 2023) in 4:22:31 and wants to break 4:00:00 at the **Colorado Marathon in Fort Collins on May 4, 2025**. He currently runs 25–28 miles per week across 4 days, with a longest recent run of 13 miles. His easy pace is roughly 9:30–10:00/mile. He has a history of **IT band syndrome** in his left knee that flared during weeks 12–14 of his last training cycle, forcing him to skip his final long run before taper. He also tends to neglect cross-training and strength work. Marcus can train 6 days per week and has access to a gym with a pool.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**RunCoach** produced:

RunCoach generated a 16-week plan starting January 6, 2025, with a target finish of 3:55:00 to build in a buffer. The plan included 5 running days per week with one cross-training day and one rest day. Long runs progressed from 14 miles in Week 1 to a peak of 22 miles in Week 13, with a 3-week taper. Speed work was introduced in Week 1 with tempo runs and intervals. Weekly mileage started at 32 miles and peaked at 52 miles in Week 12. The plan included pace targets based on a 3:55 goal (9:02/mile average, with easy runs at 9:45–10:15 and tempo runs at 8:30–8:45).

However, the plan had several problems. Mileage jumped from Marcus's current 25–28 miles to 32 miles in Week 1 — a 14–28% increase on day one. The progression from 32 to 52 peak miles included two weeks with increases exceeding 10%. There was zero mention of IT band prevention — no foam rolling protocol, no hip strengthening, no ITB-specific stretches despite this being flagged as Marcus's primary injury risk. Cross-training was listed as "30 min easy" with no specifics. The speed work in Week 1 was premature for someone who hasn't done structured intervals in months. No cutback weeks were scheduled until the taper.

**PhysioGuard** scored it **62/100** and found:

- **Dangerous initial mileage jump.** Going from 25–28 mpw to 32 mpw in Week 1 violates the 10% rule and sets up an injury from day one. The first 2 weeks should be at or below current mileage to establish the training baseline.
- **No IT band prevention protocol.** This is the most critical omission. Marcus's history of ITBS requires a specific prehab routine: hip abductor strengthening (clamshells, side-lying leg raises), glute activation (bridges, single-leg deadlifts), and foam rolling of the IT band and TFL. This should be prescribed for every strength day and pre-run.
- **Missing cutback weeks.** Every 3rd or 4th week should be a recovery week with 20–25% reduced mileage. The plan has none until the taper — a recipe for accumulated fatigue and overuse injury.
- **Speed work introduced too early.** Marcus hasn't done structured interval training. Tempo runs in Week 1 add intensity to an already-increased volume. Build the aerobic base for 3–4 weeks before introducing speed work.
- **Cross-training is vague.** "30 min easy" doesn't help. Given Marcus's access to a pool and his IT band history, pool running and swimming should be specifically prescribed as low-impact aerobic supplements. Cycling is also appropriate.
- **No race-day fueling or pacing strategy.** A sub-4:00 attempt requires a specific pacing plan (negative split vs. even split) and nutrition strategy (gels, hydration timing). These are absent.

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**PaceCalculator** addressed the feedback:

The revised plan was fundamentally restructured. Weeks 1–2 were set at 26–28 miles (matching current fitness) to establish the training base. Cutback weeks were added every 4th week with a 20–25% reduction in volume. Peak mileage was adjusted to 48 miles in Week 13 (down from 52) to reduce injury risk. Speed work was delayed until Week 4, starting with strides and progressing to tempo runs before intervals. A comprehensive IT band prehab protocol was embedded into every strength day, including specific exercises with sets, reps, and progression. Cross-training days now specified pool running (30–40 min) or swimming (20–30 min) as primary options. A detailed race-day strategy was added covering pacing, nutrition, and contingency plans. The taper was extended to a proper 3-week protocol with mileage reductions of 25%, 40%, and 60%.

**PhysioGuard** scored it **94/100**: "Meets quality threshold."

## IT Band Prehab Protocol

⚠️ **This is non-negotiable.** Your IT band history means this protocol is as important as your running.

### Pre-Run Activation (5 minutes, every run)

1. **Clamshells** — 15 reps each side, with resistance band above knees
2. **Lateral band walks** — 10 steps each direction
3. **Single-leg glute bridges** — 10 reps each side
4. **Leg swings** — 10 forward/back + 10 lateral, each leg

### Strength A Routine (Weeks 1–4, 8, 12) — 25 minutes

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Clamshells (banded) | 3 × 15 each side | Slow and controlled. Feel the glute medius. |
| Side-lying leg raises | 3 × 12 each side | Keep hips stacked. Don't roll back. |
| Glute bridges (banded) | 3 × 15 | Squeeze at top for 2 seconds. |
| Single-leg deadlifts | 3 × 10 each side | Bodyweight or light dumbbell (10–15 lb). |
| Plank | 3 × 45 sec | Core stability supports hip alignment. |
| Foam roll IT band + TFL | 5 min total | Roll slowly. Pause on tender spots for 20 sec. |

### Strength B Routine (Weeks 5–11, 13) — 30 minutes

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Clamshells (banded, heavier band) | 3 × 15 each side | Progress the band resistance. |
| Bulgarian split squats | 3 × 10 each side | Bodyweight or holding 10–15 lb dumbbells. |
| Single-leg deadlifts | 3 × 10 each side | Progress to 15–20 lb dumbbell. |
| Copenhagen plank (adductors) | 3 × 20 sec each side | Inner thigh strength supports knee stability. |
| Step-ups (12-inch box) | 3 × 12 each side | Controlled descent — don't slam down. |
| Monster walks (banded) | 2 × 20 steps each direction | Low athletic stance. |
| Foam roll IT band + TFL + glutes | 5 min total | |

### 🚨 IT Band Flare-Up Protocol

If you feel IT band tightness during a run:

1. **Stop immediately.** Do not run through it.
2. Walk for 5 minutes. If it resolves, jog lightly for 2 minutes. If tightness returns, walk home.
3. **Ice for 15 minutes** after returning.
4. **Skip the next day's run.** Replace with pool running at easy effort.
5. **Double your foam rolling and Strength A routine** for the next 3 days.
6. If pain persists beyond 3 days, see a sports PT before resuming running.
7. When returning: drop mileage by 20% for 1 week and rebuild from there.
