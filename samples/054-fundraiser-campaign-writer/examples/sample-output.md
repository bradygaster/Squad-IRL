# Fundraiser Campaign Writer — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Maria runs a community center in a low-income neighborhood. Their 25-year-old HVAC system died in July (mid-summer heat wave), and they need $18,000 to replace it. Without AC, they can't run summer programs for 150 kids. She's launching a GoFundMe but hates fundraising and doesn't know how to write a compelling story that drives donations.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**Storyteller** produced:

"Help us replace our broken HVAC system. Our community center serves 150 kids and families. We need $18,000 to buy a new AC unit. Every donation helps. Thank you for your support."

**EmotionExpert** scored it **66/100**: Too generic, no emotional connection, doesn't explain WHY donors should care, no specific story/person to connect with, missing urgency, no breakdown of how funds are used.

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**DonorPsychologist** rewrote with a specific story (8-year-old Mia who can't attend summer camp in the heat), created emotional urgency (kids missing meals + activities during critical summer months), broke down costs transparently, added donor recognition tiers, included social proof (testimonials), and structured with clear ask and impact statement.

**EmotionExpert** scored it **93/100**: "Compelling, specific, and action-oriented."

## Final Output

# Help Bring Cool Relief to 150 Kids This Summer

## The Story

Last Tuesday, 8-year-old Mia showed up at Riverside Community Center at 8am, just like she has every summer since she was five. She was excited for arts & crafts, her friends, and lunch (for many of our kids, it's their only full meal of the day).

But this summer was different. Our 25-year-old HVAC system died on July 12th—right in the middle of a heat wave with temperatures hitting 98°F. Inside the center, it was 94°F. We had to close. Mia stood outside with her mom, confused, asking "Why can't I go to camp?"

**We've been closed for 3 weeks. 150 kids have nowhere to go.**

## Why This Matters

Riverside Community Center isn't just a building. For families in our neighborhood—where 68% of kids qualify for free lunch—we're a lifeline:

- **Daily meals**: We serve breakfast and lunch to 150 kids every summer (many don't have food security at home)  
- **Safe space**: Both parents work in 80% of our families; without us, kids are home alone  
- **Learning**: Summer slide is real; our reading and math programs keep kids on track  
- **Community**: This is where friendships are built, where kids feel safe, where families connect  

Without AC, we can't operate. It's not just uncomfortable—it's unsafe. Kids, elderly neighbors who use our space, everyone is affected.

## What We Need

**Goal: $18,000 to replace the HVAC system**

Here's exactly how your donation will be used:

- **$12,500**: New commercial HVAC unit (3-ton system, energy-efficient)  
- **$3,200**: Installation labor (licensed contractor, includes electrical work)  
- **$1,500**: Permits and inspection fees (required by city code)  
- **$800**: Emergency contingency (ductwork repairs if needed)  

**We've already received 3 quotes. This is the real cost—no padding, no overhead.**

## The Impact of Your Donation

- **$25**: Provides 1 week of meals for 1 child  
- **$100**: Covers 1 day of programming for 10 kids  
- **$500**: Sponsors 1 week of summer camp for 30 kids  
- **$1,000**: Major donor recognition (plaque in lobby)  
- **$5,000+**: Naming opportunity (HVAC system dedicated in your family's name)  

## We're Almost There

**Raised so far**: $6,200 (34% of goal)  
- Bake sale: $1,100  
- Local business donations: $3,500  
- Individual donors (38 people): $1,600  

**We need**: $11,800 more to get our kids back inside

**Timeline**: If we hit our goal by August 1st, we can install the system by August 5th and reopen for the last 3 weeks of summer. Every day we're closed, 150 kids go without meals, activities, and safe supervision.
