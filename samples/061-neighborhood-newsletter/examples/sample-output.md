# Neighborhood Newsletter — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Maria Santos is president of the Oakwood Heights Neighborhood Association in Portland, Oregon. She has 12 news items collected from residents over the past month: a new bakery opening, road construction starting next week, the community garden harvest festival, a rash of car break-ins, two neighbor spotlights, and upcoming HOA meetings. She needs to compile them into the monthly newsletter that goes out to 347 households by Friday.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**Reporter** produced:

*Oakwood Heights Community Newsletter - October 2024*

Welcome neighbors! Here's what's happening this month in our community. First, we're excited to announce that Sweet Dreams Bakery is opening on Oak Street next Tuesday. They'll have fresh bread and pastries daily.

Unfortunately, there has been an increase in car break-ins on Maple and Elm streets over the past two weeks. Please remember to lock your vehicles and remove valuables. The police department recommends parking in well-lit areas when possible.

Don't miss the Community Garden Harvest Festival on October 15th from 2-5pm! Bring the family for fresh vegetables, live music, and food trucks. All proceeds benefit the community center upgrades.

**Editor** scored it **68/100** and found:

- Opening lacks warmth and community voice — reads too clinical
- Missing critical details: bakery address, festival location, what to bring
- Car break-in section feels fear-mongering without actionable solutions or community resources
- No spotlight on community members mentioned in input
- Missing HOA meeting information and how residents can contribute news
- Road construction details completely omitted despite being urgent need-to-know
- No contact information or submission guidelines for next month's newsletter

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**CommunityVoice** addressed the feedback:

*Newsletter now opens with "Hello Oakwood Heights family!" and includes personal note from Maria about community resilience during construction period. Added specific address for bakery (247 Oak Street), festival location (Community Garden, corner of Pine & 5th), and brought-item suggestions. Expanded safety section with Neighborhood Watch meeting info and non-emergency police number. Added two neighbor spotlights: retired teacher Mr. Chen's 40 years in the neighborhood and the Williams family's Little Free Library installation. Included full road construction timeline for Maple Street (Oct 12-Nov 8) with detour map description. Added community calendar section with three upcoming HOA meetings and contact email for newsletter submissions.*

**Editor** scored it **93/100**: "Excellent community voice and comprehensive coverage. Meets quality threshold."

## A Message From Your Neighborhood Association

Hello Oakwood Heights family!

October brings falling leaves, cooler weather, and quite a bit of activity in our little corner of Portland! I know road construction can be frustrating, but I'm constantly amazed by how our community comes together during challenges. Whether it's the new Little Free Library on Cedar Street or neighbors checking in on elderly residents, you all make Oakwood Heights special.

This month's newsletter is packed with updates, so grab your coffee and let's dive in!

*— Maria Santos, Neighborhood Association President*

## 🎉 COMMUNITY SPOTLIGHT: Meet Your Neighbors

**Celebrating 40 Years: Mr. David Chen**

Long-time resident David Chen is celebrating 40 years in Oakwood Heights this month! The retired Lincoln Elementary teacher has lived in the same house on Birch Street since 1984 and has taught three generations of neighborhood children. "I've watched this community grow from young families to grandparents," David shares. "The connections we've built here are irreplaceable." Stop by and say hello when you see him tending his famous rose garden!

**New Little Free Library on Cedar Street**

The Williams family (Cedar & 6th) just installed our neighborhood's second Little Free Library! Jennifer, Marcus, and their kids built it together and stocked it with 50 books. "We wanted to create a space where neighbors could share stories," says Jennifer. Stop by anytime to take a book or leave one. Kids' books especially welcome!

## 🍞 NEW IN THE NEIGHBORHOOD

**Sweet Dreams Bakery Opens This Tuesday!**

Get ready for the smell of fresh bread, Oakwood Heights! Sweet Dreams Bakery opens its doors at **247 Oak Street** (between 2nd and 3rd) on **Tuesday, October 10th at 7:00 AM**.

Owner Patricia Lee, who trained in Paris and worked at Portland's beloved Flour & Co for eight years, is bringing artisan sourdough, croissants, cinnamon rolls, and seasonal pastries to our neighborhood. Opening week special: free coffee with any purchase!

**Hours:** Tuesday-Sunday, 7am-5pm  
**Grand Opening:** Saturday, Oct 14th with samples and 20% off

## 🚧 IMPORTANT: MAPLE STREET CONSTRUCTION

**Heads up!** Maple Street between Oak and Pine will be **closed for road repair** from **October 12th through November 8th** (approximately 4 weeks).

**What to know:**
- Full closure — no through traffic
- Access maintained for residents living on that block
- Detour route: Use Elm Street or Cedar Street as alternatives
- Expect noise 7am-5pm weekdays
- Water service will be briefly interrupted Oct 19th (8am-2pm) — City will notify affected homes directly

The City is replacing 50-year-old water mains and repaving. It'll be noisy and inconvenient, but we'll have smooth roads and reliable water for years to come! Please be patient with construction crews and drive carefully in the detour areas.

**Questions?** Contact Portland Transportation: (503) 823-5185

## 🚨 SAFETY ALERT: Vehicle Break-Ins

We've had **6 reported car break-ins** on Maple and Elm Streets in the past two weeks. Portland Police believe it's the same group targeting multiple neighborhoods.

**Protect yourself:**
✓ Lock your car EVERY TIME (even in your driveway)  
✓ Remove ALL valuables from sight — even loose change attracts thieves  
✓ Park in well-lit areas when possible  
✓ Consider a steering wheel lock (visible deterrent)  
✓ Report suspicious activity immediately  

**Resources:**
- Non-emergency police: (503) 823-3333
- Online reporting: portlandoregon.gov/police
- Join our Neighborhood Watch: Next meeting **Oct 18th, 7pm** at Community Center

Remember: We look out for each other here. If you see something, say something.
