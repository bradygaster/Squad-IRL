## PHASE 1: BUILD FOUNDATIONS (Months 0-6)
**Goal:** Gain PM-adjacent skills while staying in current role

### Skills to Acquire

**1. SQL & Data Analysis (CRITICAL)**
Product Managers live in data. You need to query databases, analyze metrics, and make data-driven decisions.

**Learning Path:**
- **Course:** Mode Analytics SQL Tutorial (free, 4-6 weeks)
- **Practice:** Ask your engineering team for read-only database access. Start writing queries to answer your own CS questions.
- **Project:** Create a dashboard tracking your top customer pain points using SQL + Google Sheets

**Time investment:** 3-4 hours/week for 8 weeks

**2. Product Analytics Tools**
Learn the tools PMs use daily.

**Tools to Learn:**
- **Mixpanel or Amplitude** (event tracking)
- **Looker or Tableau** (visualization)
- **Google Analytics** (web analytics)

**How:** Ask your company''s PM team if you can shadow them during data analysis sessions. Offer to create reports for them using these tools.

**Time investment:** 2 hours/week for 12 weeks

**3. PM Frameworks & Mental Models**
Learn how PMs think and prioritize.

**Key Frameworks:**
- **RICE scoring** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort)
- **Jobs to be Done** (JTBD framework)
- **Kano Model** (feature categorization)
- **OKRs** (Objectives & Key Results)

**Resource:** "Escaping the Build Trap" by Melissa Perri (book, $18)

**Time investment:** 1 hour/week reading + applying to your CS work

**4. Basic Technical Literacy**
You don''t need to code, but you need to speak engineer.

**Learn:**
- How APIs work (basics)
- Frontend vs. backend concepts
- What a database schema is
- Agile/Scrum terminology

**Resource:** "The Non-Technical Person''s Guide to Building Products and Working with Engineers" (free online)

**Time investment:** 2 hours/week for 6 weeks

### Actions to Take IN YOUR CURRENT JOB

**Start Acting Like a PM Before You Are One:**

1. **Create a "Voice of Customer" report** → Monthly summary of customer feedback themes sent to the PM team (you''re building relationships + showing PM thinking)

2. **Propose a feature based on customer data** → Use SQL to show how many customers request X, quantify business impact, present to PMs

3. **Run a beta test** → Volunteer to coordinate user testing for an upcoming feature (PMs do this constantly)

4. **Join product meetings** → Ask your manager if you can sit in on product roadmap planning (even as an observer)

**Why this matters:** When you interview for PM roles later, you''ll have PM-like projects to discuss.

## COMMON CHALLENGES & HOW TO OVERCOME THEM

### Challenge 1: "I don''t have a technical degree"

**Reality:** 30% of PMs don''t have technical degrees (per ProductHired study)  
**Solution:** Compensate with demonstrated technical curiosity. Learn SQL, understand APIs, speak engineer. The cert programs and self-study plan above cover this.

### Challenge 2: "I''m 29, competing with 24-year-olds from Stanford"

**Reality:** Your 5 years of work experience is an ADVANTAGE over new grads  
**Solution:** Target companies that value customer insight and cross-functional skills over pedigree. Startups > big tech for your profile.

### Challenge 3: "I keep getting rejected for PM roles"

**Reality:** CS → PM conversion takes 10-20 applications on average (if you''re strategic)  
**Solution:** Stop applying blindly. Network into roles. Get referrals. One warm intro > 50 cold applications.

### Challenge 4: "My current manager won''t support my transition"

**Reality:** Some managers block internal moves because they don''t want to lose good people  
**Solution:** Frame it as "I want to grow at this company" not "I want to leave CS." If they still block you, external move is your path.
