# Wedding Timeline Builder — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Aisha and Jordan are getting married April 12 at 4pm. Ceremony and reception at the same venue (The Grove, capacity 150 guests). Timeline needed for: photographer, DJ, florist, catering staff, wedding party (6 bridesmaids, 5 groomsmen), and themselves. They want everything to run smoothly with no awkward gaps or delays.

## What the Agents Did

First draft: "3:00pm guests arrive, 4:00pm ceremony starts, 5:00pm cocktail hour, 6:00pm reception." Way too vague! No buffer time, no vendor coordination details, didn't account for the reality that family photos take 30+ minutes and would delay cocktail hour. 

Revised timeline adds critical details: 3:30pm guest arrival (people are ALWAYS early), 4:15pm actual ceremony start (coordinator needs time to seat everyone and handle last-minute issues — ceremonies never start exactly on time), 5:30pm family photos happen DURING cocktail hour (so reception can start on time at 6:30pm), 20-minute buffer before grand entrance in case photos run over.

## Final Output

Minute-by-minute timeline from 12:00pm (bride/groom arrive for hair/makeup) to 11:00pm (venue closes). Includes vendor arrival times (florist 2pm for ceremony setup, DJ 3pm for sound check, photographer 1pm for getting-ready photos, caterers 4pm for cocktail hour setup), wedding party schedule (bridesmaids hair starts 1pm in bridal suite, groomsmen arrive 3pm for photos), family photo shot list with time allocations (15 min immediate family both sides, 15 min extended family), reception timeline with specific songs/moments (grand entrance 6:20pm to "Celebration" by Kool & The Gang, first dance 6:25pm, parent dances 6:30pm, toasts 6:35pm, dinner service 7:00pm, cake cutting 8:30pm, bouquet toss 9:15pm, last dance 10:45pm), and contingency plans (if ceremony runs 10 min late, skip anniversary dance to stay on schedule).

**Aisha and Jordan's wedding ran like clockwork. The photographer said it was the most organized timeline she'd worked with all year. Zero stress, all joy.**
