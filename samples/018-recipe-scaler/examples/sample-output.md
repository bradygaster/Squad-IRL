# Recipe Scaler — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Nina's making her grandma's famous lasagna for Thanksgiving. The recipe serves 8, but she's hosting 24 people (needs to triple it). Original recipe calls for a "9x13 pan" and "bake 45 minutes at 375°F." She needs to know: Will 3 pans fit in her oven? How does baking time change? Any ingredient adjustments needed?

## What the Agents Did

First draft just tripled every ingredient (1 lb ground beef → 3 lbs, etc.) but didn't address the practical questions: baking time, oven logistics, prep workflow. Revised plan thinks through the entire process: Use 3 separate 9x13 pans (they'll fit in a standard 30" oven with 2 on the top rack, 1 on the bottom rack — rotate positions halfway through). Baking time increases to 55-60 minutes (not just 45) because multiple pans reduce oven efficiency. 

Critical detail: 3 lbs of ground beef can't be browned all at once — it needs to be done in 3 batches (overcrowding the pan will cause it to steam instead of brown, making it gray and rubbery). Also flagged that 24 lasagna noodles need a LARGE pot or done in 2 batches.

## Final Output

Scaled recipe for 24 servings with adjusted ingredient quantities (3 lbs ground beef, 6 cups ricotta, 9 cups mozzarella, 48 oz marinara, 24 noodles), modified baking instructions (55-60 min at 375°F, rotate pan positions at 30 min mark, check center with thermometer for 165°F), equipment needs (3 9x13 pans, large stock pot), and realistic prep timeline: brown meat the day before (store in fridge), assemble lasagnas the morning of (takes 90 min), bake 2 hours before dinner to allow for cooling/slicing. **Nina's triple-batch lasagna was the star of Thanksgiving. All 24 guests asked for the recipe. She had exactly 2 servings of leftovers.**
