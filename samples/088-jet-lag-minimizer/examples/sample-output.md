Light is the **#1 circadian synchronizer**. For an eastward phase advance, the rules are:

| Goal | Timing |
|---|---|

| **Avoid bright light** | In the 4–5 hours before your target bedtime |
| **Critical window** | The 2 hours after your core body temperature minimum (CBTmin) — light here causes maximum phase advance |

## ⚠️ WHAT NOT TO DO

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---|---|
| ❌ **Sleeping in on arrival day** | Sleeping past 7:00 AM JST delays your circadian shift and wastes the critical morning light window |
| ❌ **Taking a 3-hour nap after landing** | Long naps cause sleep inertia and make it impossible to sleep at the target bedtime. Max nap: 20 minutes |
| ❌ **Drinking alcohol on the plane** | Alcohol fragments sleep, causes dehydration, and suppresses REM sleep — the opposite of what you need |
| ❌ **Using sleeping pills (Ambien, etc.)** | They produce unconsciousness, not restorative sleep. They don't shift the circadian clock. Hangover effect impairs next-day cognition |
| ❌ **Staying in the hotel room all day** | Indoor light is 10–50x weaker than outdoor light. You must get outside, especially in the morning |
| ❌ **"I'll just power through with coffee"** | Caffeine masks sleepiness but does not fix circadian misalignment. Overuse causes jitters, crashes, and impaired executive function — the worst outcome for a client presentation |
| ❌ **Taking 5+ mg melatonin** | High-dose melatonin causes grogginess and is no more effective for circadian shifting than 0.3–0.5 mg |
| ❌ **Bright screen time in bed** | Suppresses natural melatonin production at exactly the wrong moment. Use night mode or put the phone away |
| ❌ **Eating a huge meal at 11 PM JST** | Late heavy meals shift the gut clock away from the destination schedule and disrupt sleep quality |
| ❌ **Exercising intensely in the evening** | Raises core body temperature and delays sleep onset. Morning exercise is ideal; evening should be light stretching only |
