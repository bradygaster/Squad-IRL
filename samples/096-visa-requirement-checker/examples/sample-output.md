## 💼 Remote Work Advisory

Amara, you mentioned you might do some freelance graphic design work while in Vietnam. Here's what you need to know:

### What Your Tourist E-Visa Allows
- Sightseeing, visiting friends, attending cultural events
- Checking email, responding to messages (normal travel communication)
- **It does not authorize any form of employment or paid work activity in Vietnam**

### The Gray Area of Remote Work
Remote freelance work — where you're working for US-based clients, being paid into a US bank account, and not engaging with the Vietnamese labor market — falls into a legal gray area in Vietnam. In practice:

- **Vietnam's labor laws** technically require a work permit for any work activity conducted on Vietnamese soil, regardless of who the employer/client is
- **Enforcement reality:** Vietnam does not actively monitor tourists working on laptops in cafes. Many digital nomads work remotely from Vietnam without incident.
- **Risk level:** Low for a 3-week trip doing quiet freelance work on a laptop. You're not taking a job from a Vietnamese worker, not earning Vietnamese dong, and not interacting with Vietnamese businesses as a service provider.

### Recommendations
1. **Do not mention remote work to immigration officers.** When asked the purpose of your visit, say "tourism." This is not deceptive — tourism is your primary purpose.
2. **Do not invoice Vietnamese clients or businesses** during your stay.
3. **Keep it low-key.** Working from your hotel room or a quiet café is fine. Don't set up in a coworking space that requires registration or a local business address.
4. **If you plan to work from Vietnam long-term in the future** (3+ months), look into Vietnam's emerging digital nomad visa discussions or a proper business visa. For a 3-week trip, this isn't necessary.

### If You Want Full Legal Coverage
The only way to be 100% legally compliant for work activities in Vietnam is a **business visa (DN visa)** combined with a work permit. This is expensive, time-consuming, and frankly overkill for a 3-week freelance trip. It's worth knowing about, but not worth pursuing for this trip.

## ❌ Common Rejection Reasons & How to Avoid Them

Vietnam e-visa applications get rejected more often than you'd think. Here are the most common reasons and how to sidestep them:

### 1. Passport Validity Under 6 Months
**Why it happens:** Travelers don't check the 6-month rule. They see a passport that isn't expired and assume it's fine.
**How to avoid:** You're already handling this with your passport renewal. Make sure the new passport has at least 6 months of validity from October 5, 2024.

### 2. Blurry or Non-Compliant Photo Upload
**Why it happens:** Selfies, photos with colored backgrounds, photos with glasses, or images taken in poor lighting.
**How to avoid:** Get a professional passport photo taken. Upload the digital version, not a photo-of-a-photo. Check that the file is JPEG format and under 2MB.

### 3. Name Mismatch Between Application and Passport
**Why it happens:** Using nicknames, middle names inconsistently, or typos.
**How to avoid:** Enter your name **exactly** as it appears on your passport data page. If your passport says "AMARA OSEI" with no middle name, enter exactly that. Don't add or omit anything.

### 4. Incorrect Passport Number
**Why it happens:** Confusing the letter "O" with zero, "I" with "1", or simple typos.
**How to avoid:** Double-check every character of your passport number against the physical document. US passport numbers are 9 characters — typically one letter followed by 8 digits.

### 5. Passport Data Page Scan Is Unreadable
**Why it happens:** Glare from phone flash, shadows, cropped edges, or low resolution.
**How to avoid:** Place your passport on a dark, flat surface. Use natural light or indirect lighting. Make sure all four edges of the data page are visible. Take the photo straight-on, not at an angle.

### 6. Entry/Exit Points Don't Match Actual Travel
**Why it happens:** Changing flights or itinerary after applying for the e-visa.
**How to avoid:** Finalize your flight bookings before applying. If your itinerary changes after the e-visa is issued, you may need to apply for a new one with the correct entry/exit checkpoints.

### 7. Applying on a Fake/Third-Party Website
**Why it happens:** Scam sites rank well in search results and look official.
**How to avoid:** Use **only** the official portal: [https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn](https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn). Bookmark it now. If a site charges more than $25 for a single-entry e-visa, it's a third party adding markup.

## ✈️ Airport Arrival Tips — Tân Sơn Nhất International Airport (SGN)

You're flying into Ho Chi Minh City, so here's what to expect at Tân Sơn Nhất:

1. **Join the "Foreign Passport" line** — not the Vietnamese citizens line
2. Have ready in your hands: passport + printed e-visa (color copy)
3. The immigration officer will scan your passport, check your e-visa, and may ask basic questions:
   - "What is the purpose of your visit?" → "Tourism"
   - "How long are you staying?" → "Three weeks"
   - "Where are you staying?" → Give your first hotel name and address
4. They'll stamp your passport with an entry stamp. **Check the stamp** — make sure the date is correct and the permitted stay duration matches your e-visa
5. Processing takes 1–5 minutes per person. The overall line can take 20–60 minutes depending on time of day.

### After Immigration
- **Baggage claim** is one floor down from immigration
- **Currency exchange** — counters are available in arrivals. The Vietnamese đồng (VND) exchange rate is roughly 24,000–25,000 VND to $1 USD. Airport rates are slightly worse than city rates, but fine for getting initial cash.
- **SIM cards** — buy a prepaid Vietnamese SIM card at the airport (Viettel, Mobifone, or Vinaphone). Around $5–10 for a tourist SIM with data. You'll need internet access for maps, translation, and ride-hailing apps.
- **Transportation to your hotel:**
  - **Grab** (the Southeast Asian Uber) is the easiest option. Download the app before you arrive. Book from the designated pickup area.
  - **Metered taxi:** Only use Mai Linh (green) or Vinasun (white). Other taxi companies may overcharge tourists.
  - **Airport bus:** Route 109 goes to the city center for 20,000 VND (~$0.80). Cheap but slower.

### Pro Tips
- Tân Sơn Nhất is a busy airport — arrivals can feel chaotic. Stay calm, follow the signs, and you'll be fine
- Fill out the arrival/departure card on the plane if one is distributed (some airlines hand these out, though Vietnam has been phasing them out for e-visa holders)
- Keep your passport and e-visa in your carry-on bag, not in checked luggage — this should go without saying, but it happens
- The airport WiFi is free but slow. Your new SIM card will be more reliable
