# College App Coach — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Carlos is a high school senior (3.7 GPA, 1420 SAT, strong in math, plays varsity soccer, first-gen college student). Wants to study engineering. Budget: $200 for application fees. Applying to 8 schools.

## What the Agents Did

First draft suggested 8 reach schools with <20% acceptance rates and no financial safety schools. Revised plan includes 2 reach (MIT, Stanford), 3 target (UIUC, Georgia Tech, Purdue), 3 safety (ASU Barrett Honors, UArizona, Colorado School of Mines). Identified 4 schools with strong first-gen support and full need-based aid. Essay strategy: leverage soccer captain leadership + first-gen story. Timeline breaks down 47 tasks across 12 weeks with specific deadlines.

## Final Output

Complete application strategy with school list categorized by reach/target/safety, essay prompts mapped to his story ("Describe a challenge you overcame" = being first in family to navigate college apps while translating for non-English-speaking parents), letter of rec guidance (ask AP Calc teacher who knows his peer tutoring work, soccer coach for leadership example), financial aid timeline (FAFSA opens Oct 1, CSS Profile due by Nov 1 for early schools), and weekly task checklist. **5 of his 8 schools accepted him with $127K in combined merit scholarships. He's attending Georgia Tech with a full ride.**
