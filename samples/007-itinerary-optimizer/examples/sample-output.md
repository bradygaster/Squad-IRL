## **DAY 1 — FRIDAY, MARCH 15: ARRIVAL & SHIBUYA**

### Morning/Early Afternoon
**10:00 AM** — Check into hotel, drop bags, freshen up  
**11:30 AM** — Brunch at **Streamer Coffee Company** (Shibuya)  
- Why: Excellent coffee, Instagram-worthy pancakes, chill vibes to ease into jet lag  
- Cost: ¥3,000 (~$20) for two  
- Allergy note: No shellfish dishes, easy to customize

### Afternoon
**1:00 PM** — Explore **Shibuya Crossing & Hachiko Statue**  
- Free! Take the obligatory photos, people-watch  
- Pro tip: View crossing from 2nd floor Starbucks for aerial perspective

**2:00 PM** — Shop **Shibuya 109** and surrounding streets  
- Budget: ¥10,000 (~$67) for shopping/souvenirs  
- Hidden gem: **Tokyu Hands** (8-floor creative lifestyle store)

**4:00 PM** — BUFFER TIME: Rest at hotel or wander Shibuya side streets

### Evening
**6:00 PM** — Dinner at **Uobei Shibuya** (conveyor belt sushi)  
- Why: Fun, affordable, fresh sushi on a literal conveyor belt  
- Cost: ¥4,000 (~$27)  
- Allergy note: Avoid shellfish plates (clearly labeled), stick to salmon/tuna/vegetarian options

**8:00 PM** — Nightcap at **Nonbei Yokocho** (tiny alley of 40+ bars)  
- Why: Local vibe, affordable drinks (¥500-800/drink), great atmosphere  
- Cost: ¥3,000 (~$20)

**Running Budget: $134 (Day 1)**

## **DAY 2 — SATURDAY, MARCH 16: ASAKUSA & TRADITIONAL TOKYO**

### Morning
**10:30 AM** — Brunch at **Café Byron Bay** (near hotel)  
- Why: Australian-style brunch, excellent avocado toast  
- Cost: ¥3,500 (~$23)

**12:00 PM** — Train to **Asakusa** (30 min, ¥200 each = ¥400 / $3)

### Afternoon (Clustered in Asakusa Area)
**12:30 PM** — **Sensō-ji Temple** (Tokyo's oldest temple)  
- Free! Walk through Kaminarimon Gate, explore Nakamise Shopping Street  
- Budget: ¥5,000 (~$34) for snacks and souvenirs on Nakamise Street

**2:30 PM** — **Sumida River Walk** to Tokyo Skytree (20 min walk)  
- Free! Scenic riverside path

**3:00 PM** — BUFFER TIME: Rest at **Sumida Park** or grab coffee

**4:30 PM** — **Tokyo Skytree** observation deck  
- Cost: ¥3,100/person = ¥6,200 (~$41)  
- Backup: If weather is bad, skip and visit **Sumida Aquarium** instead (¥2,300/person)

### Evening
**6:30 PM** — Dinner at **Sometaro** (okonomiyaki — savory pancakes)  
- Why: Local favorite, interactive cooking experience  
- Cost: ¥4,500 (~$30)  
- Allergy note: Order without seafood toppings (plenty of meat/veggie options)

**8:30 PM** — Back to hotel via train (¥400 / $3)

**Running Budget: $268 (Day 1+2 = $402 total)**

## **DAY 4 — MONDAY, MARCH 18: TSUKIJI & GINZA**

### Morning
**9:00 AM** — Early start for **Tsukiji Outer Market** (food stalls)  
- Why: Best street food in Tokyo, manageable crowds by 9am  
- Budget: ¥6,000 (~$40) for breakfast/snacks  
- Allergy note: MANY shellfish stalls — Mia should stick to tamago (egg), wagyu beef skewers, fruit stalls

**11:00 AM** — Walk to **Ginza** (20 min walk from Tsukiji)

### Afternoon
**11:30 AM** — Window shop **Ginza** (luxury district)  
- Free! Beautiful architecture, high-end stores, people-watching  
- Hidden gem: **Itoya** (8-floor stationery wonderland)

**1:00 PM** — Lunch at **Ginza Kagari** (Michelin-rated ramen)  
- Cost: ¥2,500 (~$17)

**2:30 PM** — **Tsukiji Honganji Temple** (unique Indian-style Buddhist temple)  
- Free! 15-min visit, architectural gem

**3:00 PM** — BUFFER TIME: Rest at hotel or explore side streets

### Evening
**6:00 PM** — Dinner at **Nabezo** (hot pot, Ginza)  
- Why: All-you-can-eat shabu-shabu, customizable broth  
- Cost: ¥6,000 (~$40)  
- Allergy note: Order pork/beef broth (no seafood stock), plenty of veggies/meat

**8:30 PM** — Nighttime stroll through **Ginza** (beautifully lit)

**Running Budget: $97 (Total: $610)**
