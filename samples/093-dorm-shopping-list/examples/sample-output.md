### Understanding Michigan Winter (For Someone Who's Never Seen Snow)

**Timeline of what to expect:**
- **September–October:** Beautiful. 50–70°F. Enjoy it. This is when you'll think everyone was exaggerating.
- **November:** First frost. Temperatures drop to 30s. Start wearing the thermal layers.
- **December:** Snow starts. 15–30°F. Parka goes on and doesn't come off until March.
- **January–February:** The real deal. 0–20°F, wind chill below zero. The walk to class will feel like a survival challenge. This is when the boots, gloves, and layers earn their money.
- **March:** Fake spring. It'll hit 50°F one day and 20°F the next. Don't put the coat away.
- **April:** Actual spring. You survived.

**Survival tips from upperclassmen:**
1. **Layer, don't bulk.** Thermal base → fleece mid-layer → parka shell. You can shed layers in overheated classrooms.
2. **Protect your extremities.** Frostbite hits fingers, toes, ears, and nose first. Gloves + beanie + wool socks every time.
3. **Moisturize.** Michigan winter air is BRUTALLY dry compared to Atlanta humidity. Your skin will crack. Get a good lotion (see Bathroom section).
4. **Walk on the salted paths.** UMich salts the main walkways. Stick to them. Black ice is real and invisible.
5. **The tunnel system exists.** Some North Campus buildings are connected by tunnels. Learn the routes by October.
6. **Your Atlanta rain jacket is not a winter coat.** It's not insulated, it's not windproof, it will not keep you warm at 5°F. Use the parka.
7. **Keep an emergency kit in your backpack:** hand warmers, a spare beanie, lip balm, portable charger (cold kills phone batteries).
