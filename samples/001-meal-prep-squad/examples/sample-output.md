## **RECIPES**

### **Monday: Grilled Chicken with Roasted Root Vegetables**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 lbs chicken breast  
- 3 medium carrots, peeled and chopped  
- 2 medium parsnips, peeled and chopped  
- 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets  
- 3 tbsp olive oil  
- 2 tsp garlic powder  
- 1 tsp paprika  
- Salt and pepper to taste  

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Toss vegetables with 2 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper
3. Spread on baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes
4. Meanwhile, brush chicken with remaining olive oil and season with paprika, salt, pepper
5. Grill chicken 6-7 minutes per side until internal temp reaches 165°F
6. Serve chicken alongside roasted vegetables

**Diabetes-Friendly Notes:** High protein, moderate carbs from root vegetables. Pair with a small side salad if desired.

---

### **Tuesday: Vegetarian Black Bean Chili** 🌱

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed  
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes  
- 1 can (15 oz) corn, drained  
- 1 large onion, diced  
- 1 bell pepper, diced  
- 3 cloves garlic, minced  
- 2 tbsp chili powder  
- 1 tsp cumin  
- 1 tsp oregano  
- 2 tbsp olive oil  
- Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, cilantro  

**Instructions:**
1. Heat olive oil in large pot over medium heat
2. Sauté onion and bell pepper for 5 minutes until soft
3. Add garlic, cook 1 minute
4. Add chili powder, cumin, oregano; stir for 30 seconds
5. Add tomatoes, black beans, corn
6. Bring to boil, then simmer 25-30 minutes
7. Serve with optional toppings

**Kid-Friendly Tips:** Emma might prefer this without the peppers — set aside a portion before adding them. Serve with tortilla chips on the side!

---

### **Wednesday: Chicken Fajitas**

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 lbs chicken breast, sliced into strips  
- 2 bell peppers (red and yellow), sliced  
- 1 large onion, sliced  
- 2 tbsp olive oil  
- 2 tsp chili powder  
- 1 tsp cumin  
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder  
- Juice of 1 lime  
- 10 low-carb tortillas (6-inch)  
- Optional toppings: cheese, sour cream, salsa, guacamole  

**Instructions:**
1. Mix chicken strips with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and lime juice
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in large skillet over high heat
3. Cook chicken 6-8 minutes until cooked through; set aside
4. Add remaining olive oil, cook peppers and onions 5-6 minutes
5. Return chicken to pan, toss together
6. Warm tortillas and serve with toppings

**Diabetes-Friendly Notes:** Using low-carb tortillas keeps carbs at 28g per serving. Skip the tortilla entirely for even lower carbs!

---

### **Thursday: Beef & Broccoli Stir-Fry**

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 lbs flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain  
- 4 cups broccoli florets  
- 3 cloves garlic, minced  
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced  
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce  
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce  
- 1 tbsp cornstarch  
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil  
- 1 tsp sesame oil  
- 3 cups cooked brown rice (for serving)  

**Instructions:**
1. Mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch in small bowl
2. Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in wok or large skillet over high heat
3. Stir-fry beef 3-4 minutes until browned; remove and set aside
4. Add remaining vegetable oil, stir-fry broccoli 3 minutes
5. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds
6. Return beef to pan, add sauce mixture
7. Stir-fry 2 minutes until sauce thickens
8. Drizzle with sesame oil and serve over brown rice

**Serving Note:** This makes 6 servings for Thursday night when Sarah's mom visits!

---

### **Friday: Build-Your-Own Margherita Pizza Night** 🌱

**Ingredients:**
- 5 small thin pizza crusts (store-bought or homemade)  
- 2 cups marinara sauce  
- 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese  
- 1 cup fresh basil leaves  
- 2 tbsp olive oil  
- 1 tsp garlic powder  

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 475°F
2. Place pizza crusts on baking sheets
3. Let each family member build their own: spread sauce, add cheese, sprinkle with garlic powder
4. Bake 10-12 minutes until cheese is bubbly and crust is golden
5. Top with fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil

**Kid-Friendly Tips:** Emma loves helping make her own pizza! Let the kids get creative with toppings. Keep it simple for picky eaters.

---

### **Saturday: Pork Tenderloin with Garlic Green Beans & Sweet Potato Wedges**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 lbs pork tenderloin  
- 1.5 lbs green beans, trimmed  
- 3 medium sweet potatoes, cut into wedges  
- 4 cloves garlic, minced  
- 3 tbsp olive oil  
- 1 tsp rosemary  
- Salt and pepper to taste  

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Toss sweet potato wedges with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper; spread on baking sheet
3. Roast sweet potatoes for 25 minutes
4. Meanwhile, season pork with rosemary, salt, and pepper
5. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in oven-safe skillet, sear pork 2-3 minutes per side
6. Transfer skillet to oven, roast 15-18 minutes until internal temp reaches 145°F
7. In separate pan, sauté green beans with remaining olive oil and garlic for 6-8 minutes
8. Let pork rest 5 minutes before slicing

**Diabetes-Friendly Notes:** Sweet potatoes have a lower glycemic index than white potatoes. Moderate portion sizes work well here.

---

### **Sunday: Slow Cooker Pot Roast with Carrots & Potatoes**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 lbs beef chuck roast  
- 1 lb baby carrots  
- 1.5 lbs baby potatoes, halved  
- 1 large onion, quartered  
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed  
- 2 cups beef broth  
- 2 tbsp tomato paste  
- 2 tsp thyme  
- 2 bay leaves  
- Salt and pepper to taste  

**Instructions:**
1. Season roast generously with salt and pepper
2. Optional: Sear roast in skillet for 3-4 minutes per side (adds flavor but not required)
3. Place carrots, potatoes, onion, and garlic in slow cooker
4. Place roast on top of vegetables
5. Mix beef broth with tomato paste, thyme; pour over roast
6. Add bay leaves
7. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours or HIGH for 5 hours
8. Remove bay leaves before serving

**Sunday Prep Tip:** This is your easiest dinner of the week! Put it in the slow cooker in the morning and forget about it.
