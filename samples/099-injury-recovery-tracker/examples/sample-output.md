# 🦵 INJURY PROFILE

**Diagnosis:** Grade 2 MCL Sprain — Left Knee

**What's damaged:** The medial collateral ligament runs along the inside of the knee, connecting the femur (thighbone) to the tibia (shinbone). A Grade 2 sprain means the ligament fibers are partially torn. The knee joint is structurally intact but has increased laxity (looseness) when stress-tested. Unlike a Grade 3 (complete tear), a Grade 2 typically heals without surgery.

**Healing biology:** MCL has a good blood supply compared to other knee ligaments (like the ACL), which is why it heals relatively well on its own. Collagen fibers begin remodeling around week 3 and reach functional strength around weeks 6–8, though full tissue maturity takes 3–6 months.

**What this means for Ryan:**
- The ligament *will* heal, but pushing too hard too early can re-tear fibers that are still knitting together
- No surgery expected — conservative management with PT
- The MRI ruling out ACL and meniscus damage is excellent news and simplifies the recovery path
- Ryan's age (38) and fitness level are favorable — active individuals with good baseline strength tend to recover on the faster end of the spectrum

# 📅 RECOVERY PHASES

## Phase 1: Acute Protection (Weeks 1–2) — September 28 – October 12

**Primary Goals:**
- Reduce swelling and pain
- Protect the healing ligament from valgus stress (force pushing the knee inward)
- Restore full knee extension (straightening)
- Begin gentle quad activation

**What's Happening in the Knee:**
Inflammation is the body's healing response. Blood flow increases to the area, delivering repair cells. The torn collagen fibers are being cleaned up and early scar tissue is forming. This is not the time to "push through it."

**Exercises (Daily, Non-PT Days):**

| Exercise | Description | Sets × Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|

| Ankle pumps | Flex and point foot rhythmically | 3 × 20 | Prevents blood clots, reduces swelling |
| Heel slides | Lying on back, slide heel toward buttock, bending knee as tolerated | 3 × 10 | Go only to comfortable range |

# 🔴 RED FLAGS / SETBACK WARNING SIGNS

### Call Dr. Mehta's Office Immediately If:

- **Sudden increase in swelling** — the knee balloons up after an activity or overnight for no clear reason
- **Knee gives way or buckles** — feeling of instability, the knee "shifting" during weight-bearing
- **Sharp, stabbing pain** (different from the dull ache of healing) — especially on the inner side of the knee
- **Unable to bear weight** after previously being able to — regression in weight-bearing ability
- **Locking or catching** — the knee gets stuck in one position and won't straighten or bend
- **Numbness or tingling** below the knee, in the foot, or in the calf
- **Calf pain, warmth, or swelling** — could indicate a blood clot (DVT); this is a medical emergency
- **Fever** combined with increased knee warmth and redness — possible infection (rare but serious)
- **Popping sensation** followed by immediate pain and swelling — possible re-tear or new injury

### Warning Signs That Recovery Is Off Track (Discuss with PT Jenna):

- Pain plateau — pain hasn't improved in 7+ days despite following the program
- Swelling that increases after every exercise session and doesn't resolve overnight
- ROM gains have stalled for 2+ weeks
- Significant pain difference between PT sessions (much worse day after PT)
- Compensating — you notice yourself favoring the other leg consistently
- Psychological avoidance — you're afraid to do exercises that should be safe at your phase

### What to Do If You Have a Setback:

1. **Stop the activity immediately.** Don't push through.
2. **Return to RICE:** Rest, Ice (20 min), Compression, Elevation.
3. **Log it:** Write down what happened, what you were doing, pain level, any swelling.
4. **Notify PT Jenna** before your next session so she can adjust.
5. **Do NOT skip PT.** A setback is when you need PT most.
6. **Step back one phase.** If you were in Phase 3, return to Phase 2 exercises until cleared.
7. **Don't catastrophize.** Setbacks are common in MCL recovery. One bad day doesn't reset the clock to zero.
