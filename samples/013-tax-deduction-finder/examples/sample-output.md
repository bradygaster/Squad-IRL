# Tax Deduction Finder — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Jordan is a freelance graphic designer (earned $87K in 2024) who works from home. He's never itemized deductions, just takes the standard deduction ($14,600). Has receipts for some business expenses but unsure what counts.

## What the Agents Did

First scan found obvious deductions (Adobe Creative Cloud subscription $660, new MacBook Pro $1,800) but missed home office deduction (300 sq ft dedicated workspace = $1,500 deduction using simplified method), mileage to client meetings (1,240 miles × $0.67/mile = $831), continuing education (Skillshare courses $168), and professional association fees (AIGA membership $275). Revised analysis found $9,847 in total business deductions. Also identified he's been missing quarterly estimated tax payments — owes $847 in interest/penalties.

## Final Output

Deduction summary: **$9,847 in Schedule C business expenses** (reduces both income tax AND self-employment tax). While standard deduction ($14,600) is still higher than itemized personal deductions, filing Schedule C for business expenses saves $1,379 in self-employment tax. Also found he overpaid his estimated taxes in Q4, resulting in $312 refund. **Net result: $1,691 in tax savings.** Includes 2025 tracking spreadsheet with monthly expense categories, quarterly estimated tax calculator, and mileage log template so nothing gets missed next year.
