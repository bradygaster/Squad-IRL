## ❌ WHAT TO AVOID (Well-Meaning But Wrong)

### 1. Minecraft T-Shirts or Merch
**Why to skip:** 11-year-olds are transitioning out of "character clothing." It''s too babyish for middle school. He might wear it at home but never in public.

### 2. Board Games (Unless He''s a Board Game Kid)
**Why to skip:** Most 11-year-olds in 2024 prefer video games or building activities. Board games feel like "family game night" (forced fun) unless he''s specifically into them.

### 3. Craft Kits (Slime, Tie-Dye, etc.)
**Why to skip:** These are more popular with younger kids (ages 7-9). By 11, boys are usually past the "make slime" phase.

### 4. Clothes or Shoes
**Why to skip:** Tweens are picky about brands and style. You''ll guess wrong. Cash for clothes shopping is better than clothes themselves.

### 5. Gift Cards (Unless You Have No Other Choice)
**Why to skip:** Gift cards feel impersonal from a grandparent. They''re fine from friends, but grandmas are supposed to put thought in. Use gift cards as a last resort.

## 💡 EXTRA PRO TIPS FOR GRANDPARENT GIFT-GIVING

### 1. Include a Handwritten Card
**What to write:** "I heard you''re learning to code! I thought this might be fun for us to do together when I visit this summer. Love, Grandma"

**Why it matters:** Tweens act like they don''t care about sentimental stuff, but they keep grandparent cards. (Ask me how I know.)

### 2. Text Him a Photo After You Buy It
Take a pic of the wrapped gift with a caption like "Your birthday present is ready! 🎁 Can you guess what it is?"

**Why:** It builds anticipation. He''ll be talking about it with his parents. ("What do you think Grandma got me?")

### 3. Ask His Parents First (Optional But Smart)
Quick text to your daughter: "I''m thinking of getting Tyler [the LEGO Minecraft set]. Does he already have this one? Is there anything he really wants?"

**Why:** Avoids duplicate gifts and shows you''re thoughtful. Plus, parents appreciate the check-in.

### 4. Keep the Receipt
**Why:** If Tyler already has the LEGO set or the robot doesn''t connect to his device, his parents can easily exchange it. Include the gift receipt in the card (not the price).
