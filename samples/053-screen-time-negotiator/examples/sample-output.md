# Screen Time Negotiator — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Jennifer has a 10-year-old son, Kai, who's constantly battling her about screen time. He wants "unlimited gaming" with his friends online. Jennifer wants him outside, reading, or literally doing anything else. Current situation: lots of fights, inconsistent enforcement, Kai sneaking iPad time at midnight. They need a fair agreement both sides will actually follow.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**Mediator** produced:

Kai gets 2 hours of screen time per day on weekdays, 3 hours on weekends. He must finish homework first. No screens after 8pm.

**FairnessJudge** scored it **70/100** and found:

- One-sided (parent-dictated, not negotiated—Kai won't buy in)
- Doesn't differentiate between screen types (educational vs. gaming vs. social)
- Missing consequences/rewards (what happens if he follows it? breaks it?)
- Rigid (no flexibility for special circumstances)
- Doesn't address the real conflict (Kai's social life is online with friends)

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**ContractWriter** created a collaborative agreement with Kai's input, differentiated between "productive" and "entertainment" screen time, built in earning opportunities (extra time for reading, chores), included family exceptions (movie nights, travel), addressed the friend social aspect (recognizes online gaming is how Kai connects), and created a visual tracking system with clear consequences that reset weekly.

**FairnessJudge** scored it **92/100**: "Meets quality threshold. Balanced, specific, and sustainable."

## Final Output

# Screen Time Agreement: Kai & Mom

**Effective Date**: March 18, 2024  
**Review Date**: May 18, 2024 (every 2 months, we check if it's working)

---

## Core Daily Limits

### School Nights (Mon-Thu)
**Total Screen Time**: 90 minutes  
- **Productive Time** (doesn't count against limit): Homework, educational apps, school projects  
- **Entertainment Time** (counts toward limit): Gaming, YouTube, social media, streaming

**Breakdown**:
- 60 minutes: Gaming/social time (talking to friends online, Minecraft, Roblox, etc.)  
- 30 minutes: Free choice (YouTube, TikTok, Netflix, whatever)

**Screen Curfew**: All devices off by 9:00pm (charging station in kitchen)

---

### Weekends & Fridays (Fri-Sun)
**Total Screen Time**: 3 hours  
- Same rules: Educational doesn't count, entertainment does  
- Can be split however Kai wants (all at once or broken up)

**Screen Curfew**: All devices off by 10:00pm

---

## How Kai Can Earn Extra Time

Kai can earn +15 minute bonuses for these activities (max 1 hour extra per week):

✅ **+15 min**: Read for 30 minutes (actual book, not phone)  
✅ **+15 min**: Complete a chore without being asked (dishes, trash, yard work)  
✅ **+15 min**: Play outside/active for 1 hour (bike, basketball, walk dog)  
✅ **+15 min**: Family activity participation (game night, help cook dinner, etc.)

**Tracking**: Kai tracks on a chart (fridge checklist). Mom verifies and signs off.

---

## Special Exceptions (Don't Count Against Limit)

These situations are UNLIMITED or have their own rules:

🎬 **Family Movie Night**: Friday or Saturday, we watch a movie together → doesn't count  
🚗 **Long Car Trips**: Road trips over 2 hours → unlimited screen time in car  
📱 **Texting/Calls with Family**: Calling grandparents, texting dad → doesn't count  
🏥 **Sick Days**: If Kai is home sick from school → limits are relaxed (Mom's discretion)  
🎮 **Special Gaming Events**: If there's a big tournament or special event with friends → Kai can request a "bonus day" (up to 2 per month, must ask 24 hours ahead)

---

## What Kai Gets

✅ **Predictability**: Kai always knows how much time he has left (visual timer)  
✅ **Control**: Kai chooses HOW to spend his entertainment time (gaming vs. YouTube vs. whatever)  
✅ **Earning Power**: Kai can increase his time through positive actions  
✅ **Social Connection**: Mom recognizes gaming IS how Kai hangs out with friends (it's not "wasted time")  
✅ **Weekend Freedom**: 3 hours on weekends is enough for a full gaming session with friends  
✅ **No Surprise Changes**: Mom won't suddenly take away screen time unless Kai breaks a rule

---

## What Mom Gets

✅ **Healthy Balance**: Kai spends time on non-screen activities (reading, outside, family time)  
✅ **Better Sleep**: Devices off by 9pm on school nights = better sleep quality  
✅ **No Midnight Sneaking**: Devices charge in kitchen, not Kai's room  
✅ **Homework First**: Screens only AFTER homework/chores (built-in incentive)  
✅ **Visibility**: Mom can check Kai's screen time usage on Family Link/Screen Time app  
✅ **Enforcement Clarity**: Clear consequences, not constant negotiation

---

## The Rules

### Kai Must:

1. **Finish homework and chores BEFORE entertainment screen time starts**  
   - If homework isn't done by 7pm, screen time is forfeited that day

2. **Use the timer honestly**  
   - Set a visible timer when starting screen time  
   - When timer goes off, stop immediately (no "just one more minute")

3. **Put devices in kitchen charging station by curfew**  
   - School nights: 9:00pm  
   - Weekends: 10:00pm

4. **Respectful behavior**  
   - No arguing, whining, or negotiating once screen time is over  
   - No sneaking extra time (no devices in room after curfew)

5. **Ask before downloading new apps or games**  
   - Mom reviews for age-appropriateness

---

### Mom Must:

1. **Honor the agreement**  
   - Don't randomly take away screen time unless Kai breaks a rule  
   - Don't arbitrarily shorten time because you're in a bad mood

2. **Give 10-minute warnings**  
   - When Kai has 10 minutes left, give him a heads-up (so he can finish his game/video)

3. **Acknowledge earned time**  
   - If Kai earns extra time, add it to his balance (track on chart)

4. **Be reasonable about special requests**  
   - If Kai has a legitimate reason for extra time (friend's birthday party online, special event), consider it

5. **Review and adjust every 2 months**  
   - If the agreement isn't working, we discuss and modify (this is a living document)

---

## Consequences (What Happens If Rules Are Broken)

### If Kai Breaks a Rule:

**1st Offense (in a week)**: Warning + lose 15 minutes next day  
**2nd Offense**: Lose 30 minutes next day  
**3rd Offense**: Lose all screen time next day (full 24-hour break)  
**Sneaking devices after curfew**: Automatic 48-hour screen ban (except homework)

**Reset**: Consequences reset every Monday (fresh start each week)

**Why this works**: Kai isn't "grounded forever" for one mistake. Consequences are proportional and time-limited.

---

### If Mom Breaks a Rule:

**If Mom randomly takes away screen time without Kai breaking a rule**: Kai earns +30 bonus minutes  
**If Mom forgets to give 10-minute warning**: Kai gets +5 minute grace period  

**Why this works**: Agreement holds both parties accountable. Mom can't just change the rules arbitrarily.

## Parent Tips for Success

✅ **Involve Kai in creating this**: Don't just hand him the rules. Sit down together, ask for his input, negotiate the details. He's 10—he can reason and compromise.

✅ **Use tech to enforce**: Set up Apple Screen Time or Google Family Link to automatically lock devices after time limit. Removes the emotional battle.

✅ **Praise when he follows it**: "Hey, I noticed you stopped gaming right when the timer went off. That's awesome. Thanks for respecting the agreement."

✅ **Be consistent for 2 weeks**: The first 2 weeks are hard (old habits die slowly). After that, it becomes routine.

✅ **Revisit if it's not working**: If Kai is constantly breaking rules or seems miserable, the limits might be too strict. Adjust. The goal is balance, not control.
