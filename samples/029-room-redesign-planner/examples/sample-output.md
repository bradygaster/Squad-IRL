# Room Redesign Planner — Example Output

> This is what the agent team produces when you run this sample.

## Scenario

Marcus Chen, 28, just moved into a 1-bedroom apartment in Portland, Oregon. His bedroom is 11×12 feet with one small north-facing window, dark gray walls from the previous tenant, and a popcorn ceiling. He's currently using a full-size mattress on the floor, a folding table as a desk, and stacking plastic bins for storage. He works from home 3 days a week as a UX designer and needs the room to double as an office. His budget is $2,500, and he wants the space to feel bright, modern, and productive.

## What the Agents Did

### Round 1: First Draft

**Designer** produced a room redesign plan with layout, color scheme, and shopping list:

```
ROOM REDESIGN: Marcus's Bedroom/Office Combo
- Paint walls white
- Get a bed frame and desk
- Add shelving
- Get a rug and curtains
- Estimated budget: ~$2,400
```

**SpacePlanner** scored it **62/100** and found:

- No actual furniture dimensions or placement — impossible to know if pieces fit the 11×12 room
- "Paint walls white" is vague — no specific paint color, finish, or brand recommended
- Missing lighting plan entirely — north-facing window means the room gets minimal natural light
- No ergonomic considerations for someone working from home 3 days/week
- Shopping list has no specific products, stores, or prices — just categories
- Doesn't address the popcorn ceiling issue at all

### Round 2: Improved Draft

**BudgetKeeper** addressed the feedback:

- Added a dimensioned floor plan with exact furniture placement and traffic flow paths
- Specified Benjamin Moore "Simply White" (OC-117) in eggshell finish for walls
- Created a three-layer lighting plan: overhead LED panel, desk task lamp, and ambient LED strip
- Selected an ergonomic desk chair and proper desk height for WFH setup
- Built an itemized shopping list with specific products, retailers, and prices totaling $2,487
- Added a note about ceiling options (scraping vs. covering with lightweight panels)

**SpacePlanner** scored it **93/100**: "Excellent space utilization with clear measurements. Shopping list is actionable and within budget."

**Layout Logic:**
- **Sleep Zone (West/North):** Queen bed against the west wall with headboard facing north. This keeps the bed away from the door and creates visual separation from the workspace.
- **Work Zone (South):** L-shaped desk in the south-east corner, facing the south wall. Chair has clear path to door. Bookshelf to the right for reference materials and display.
- **Storage Zone (North-West):** Freestanding wardrobe next to the window wall. Mirror mounted on the east wall opposite the window to bounce light across the room.
- **Traffic Flow:** 2.5-foot pathway between bed and desk zone allows easy movement. Door swings into the south-east, unobstructed by furniture.

## 4. Lighting Plan

The north-facing window provides soft, indirect light but never direct sunlight. This plan compensates with three lighting layers.

### Layer 1: Overhead Ambient
- **Fixture:** Hykolity 24" LED Flush Mount Panel, 3000K warm white, 2400 lumens, dimmable
- **Placement:** Center of ceiling
- **Purpose:** General room illumination; replaces the existing builder-grade dome light
- **Cost:** $36

### Layer 2: Task Lighting
- **Fixture:** BenQ ScreenBar Monitor Light, 2700–6500K adjustable
- **Placement:** Mounted on top of monitor
- **Purpose:** Illuminates desk surface without screen glare; adjustable color temperature for day/night work
- **Cost:** $109

### Layer 3: Ambient / Mood
- **Fixture:** Govee RGBIC LED Strip Lights, 16.4 ft
- **Placement:** Behind headboard and along the back edge of the desk
- **Purpose:** Warm evening ambiance; eliminates harsh shadows; makes room feel larger
- **Cost:** $16

### Layer 4: Bedside
- **Fixture:** IKEA SYMFONISK Speaker Lamp (WiFi)
- **Placement:** Nightstand
- **Purpose:** Reading light + smart speaker for alarms/music
- **Cost:** $130

**Total Lighting Cost: $291**

## 9. Pro Tips for Marcus

1. **Paint order matters:** Ceiling first, then walls, then trim. This way drips from upper surfaces get covered by lower coats.

2. **The mirror trick:** Hanging the 24×36 mirror directly across from the window effectively doubles your natural light. This single change will transform the room more than anything else.

3. **Zone your lighting with smart plugs:** Put the Govee strips and desk lamp on separate smart plugs ($8 each). Create "Work Mode" (overhead + desk lamp) and "Sleep Mode" (Govee only) scenes.

4. **Desk orientation:** Facing the south wall (not the window) prevents glare on your monitor and eliminates the distraction of looking outside during video calls. Your background on calls will be the neatly made bed and art — professional but personal.

5. **Cable management first:** Run the J Channel raceway along the desk edge and down the wall *before* placing anything on the desk. It's 10× harder to do after.

6. **Wardrobe lighting hack:** Stick a $7 motion-sensing LED bar inside the PAX wardrobe. IKEA sells them specifically for PAX units (STÖTTA).

7. **Future upgrade path:** If budget allows later, replace the flush mount with a ceiling fan + light combo ($120–180). North-facing rooms tend to feel cool, and air circulation helps.
